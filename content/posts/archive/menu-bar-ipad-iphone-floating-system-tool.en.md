Menu Bar is a lightweight floating system utility designed for iPad and iPhone. Built on Picture in Picture, it keeps network speed, CPU usage, memory usage, clipboard information, and other status signals visible in a compact floating window, bringing a desktop-like menu bar experience to mobile devices.

![Menu Bar floating status panel on iPad Pro](https://cdn.omegaxyz.com/2026/05/menu-bar/menu-bar-ipad-pro-floating-dashboard.png)

*Menu Bar presents system status in a compact floating panel on iPad.*

For developers, power users, and anyone who wants a clearer view of device activity, Menu Bar offers a more direct and less intrusive way to monitor what is happening. You do not need to switch apps repeatedly or dig through system settings. Network activity, performance indicators, clipboard history, and selected widgets can stay near the edge of the screen, ready for a quick glance.

## Key Features

- Real-time network upload and download speed
- CPU and memory usage monitoring
- Picture in Picture floating display
- Clipboard history
- Custom display components
- Saved templates and quick switching
- Minimal design with a low-distraction interface
- Local-first operation with privacy in mind

## A Persistent Status Bar for Mobile Workflows

On iPad and iPhone, useful status information is often scattered across different places. Network speed, system load, clipboard content, now playing information, and calendar-related context usually require extra taps or app switching. Menu Bar turns those high-frequency signals into lightweight components and keeps them visible through a floating interface.

This makes iPad workflows feel closer to a desktop environment. While writing, debugging, working remotely, reading documents, or running long tasks, you can keep the main app in focus while still watching device and network status in real time.

## Designed to Stay Out of the Way

Menu Bar does not try to copy a full desktop utility onto a mobile screen. Instead, it keeps the part that matters most: persistent, lightweight, glanceable information. The floating window is designed to reduce visual interruption, and the amount of information is tuned for mobile displays.

With saved templates and quick switching, you can prepare different layouts for different contexts. For example, you can focus on upload and download speed while debugging a connection, keep clipboard history visible while collecting references, or use a minimal status layout for everyday work.

## Privacy and Permissions

Menu Bar runs locally by default, and all data is processed on the device by default. Some features may require background permissions or clipboard access in order to provide floating display, clipboard history, or system status functionality.
