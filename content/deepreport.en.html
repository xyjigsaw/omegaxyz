DeepReport is an AI-assisted idea generation system for scientific research. It addresses a practical bottleneck in modern scholarship: the literature grows faster than any researcher can read, connect, and synthesize, while many valuable interdisciplinary ideas live between concepts that rarely appear together in one narrow reading list.

What DeepReport Does

From a product perspective, DeepReport supports paper retrieval, insight extraction, concept exploration, knowledge summarization, and idea generation. It is not only a summary interface over papers. Its core task is closer to a two-stage research assistant: first find promising future links between academic concepts, then use large language models to verbalize those structured signals into ideas that researchers can inspect, challenge, and refine.

The paper DeepReport: An AI-assisted Idea Generation System for Scientific Research was accepted to the SIGIR 2025 demo track. The system described in the paper maintains evolving concept co-occurrence graphs over more than 260 million publications across disciplines, updates concepts and relations periodically, and combines temporal link prediction, graph analysis, and large language models to transform cross-domain patterns into actionable research ideas.

Why It Is Interesting

New scientific ideas rarely appear from nowhere. They often come from three operations: discovering an interpretable relation between concepts that were previously separated, judging whether that relation is becoming stronger over time, and translating it back into a concrete domain problem with testable hypotheses. DeepReport is interesting because it turns these operations into a system workflow.

This is also why the system sits naturally between academic big data and knowledge graphs. Graph structure stores concept relations and historical changes, temporal prediction surfaces possible new links, and LLMs translate machine-discovered structure into language that researchers can continue to reason with. For researchers, the tool behaves less like an oracle and more like a dense literature radar: it reduces exploration cost while leaving judgment, selection, and experimental design to humans.

Where To Start

To try the system, open the DeepReport demo. For academic details, read the ACM PDF. A concise project description is also available in the DeepReport section of the personal homepage.

I am especially interested in two next steps: continuous monitoring of emerging concept links around a research topic, and tighter coupling between generated ideas, evidence trails, counterexamples, and reproducible experiment plans. That would move idea generation from a moment of inspiration into the daily research workflow.
