The difference between software engineers and coders

IdeasDeveloper LifeIdeas
Let me digress first. First of all, people with these two titles are not repairing computers and installing software. I believe that computer assembly and maintenance (computer repair, software installation, system installation, office, basic C language operations) are essential basic skills for any office white-collar worker, and asking a programmer or software engineer to do these things is simply an insult to them.Let’s get to the point, let’s talk about the difference between software engineers and programmers. Here are some people’s opinions: A good software engineer not only considers how to use code to implement functions, but also considers the whole, the relationship between modules, the places this function affects, and efficiency and other issues. Coders, on the other hand, just code to realize the function without seriously thinking about the significance of realizing this function. ——Shan Hongwei A true software engineer needs to use his thoughts to better realize functions or requirements through coding. However, coders are mechanical conversions, and the programs they create are often lifeless, let alone the systems or software extensions they develop that are easy to use. The main difference between the two is thought and responsibility. ——Mr Xiao Good software engineers use experience and ideas to create, Coders, on the other hand, work over and over again according to fixed procedures. ——Huang Xiang Software engineers and programmers, the difference. One has his own design ideas, and the other does not; one has his own basic knowledge structure, and the other is just a tool for plagiarism and piracy; one has his own achievements and products, and the other is just helping to fight against plagiarism. It's still the same after a long time...——Mr Qi A real software engineer can think, draw inferences from one example, be full of creativity and enthusiasm, have a good computer foundation (software and hardware, network, algorithm, db, ps), and have the typical characteristics of people with an engineering background, such as: seriousness, advocating simplicity, etc. Generally, senior IT people stand out from this; ordinary software engineers (code farmers) have no overall understanding of computers. Due to the limitations of knowledge and personal qualities, they often focus on the programming language itself, resulting in the inability to open up their horizons and find the value of IT. Personally, I feel that real software engineers will still say that they are software engineers. This is a stubborn recognition of themselves and other real software engineers. ——Tan Cao IT leaders: annual income of over 100 million (such as Ren Zhengfei, Ma Huateng, Robin Li, Ding Lei, Jack Ma, etc., including income from options, stocks, investment and financial management, etc.) IT Big Brother: Annual income of tens of millions (Company bosses who are second to the above bosses are not short of money and are generally envious and jealous of the people in the previous article.) IT Elite: Make millions a year (Vice president-level figures in various IT companies, including COO, CTO, etc., most of whom are professional managers, will run away before they earn enough.) IT talents: annual income of 500,000 (director-level figures in various IT companies, own houses and cars, and have relatively low life pressure) IT engineer: annual income of 200,000 (senior manager level, with mortgage, high pressure in life) IT migrant worker: annual income of 100,000 (manager level, basically no house, learn to act cool, life is stressful) Programmer: annual income of 60,000 to 100,000 (work for three or four years, rent a house, and continue to live a life) Code slave: annual income of 30,000 to 60,000 (work for one or two years, rent a house, and hang out) Code animal: Annual income is less than 30,000 (fresh graduate, renting, silly fun) Remember never to be a programmer or coder.
