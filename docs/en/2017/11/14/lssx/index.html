Do you need to learn discrete mathematics to program?

Technology其它Ideas离散数学
I see that many people search my website for discrete mathematics. Let’s talk about discrete mathematics. Discrete mathematics The object of study in Discrete mathematics is discrete. What is discrete? Discrete is relative to continuous, and discrete means that it cannot be divided infinitely. So what is discrete in the real world? There are many examples that can be cited. Generally speaking, discrete things include processes and stages. Everything can be divided into steps, or can be counted, etc. If you carefully review the content of discrete mathematics, you will find some problems like this. In logic, the unit of propositional logic is the smallest proposition, and these propositions have obvious discrete characteristics and cannot be divided. The number of propositions can be clearly counted. So the units that make up propositional logic are discrete. When we get to first-order logic, we go deep into the interior of propositional logic and study the elements that constitute propositions. It can be said that we have further divided propositions. However, this division still does not change its discrete nature and cannot be infinitely divided, and thus cannot achieve the level of continuity. As mentioned in algebra, the Cartesian product is very important because its existence depicts the space in which a thing may exist. There are many things in this space. Some things are even completely opposite to those in our real life, but they are also consistent with common sense. The reason why there are things in real life that we cannot see in the Descartes product is because this world adds restrictions on the basis of the Descartes product, thereby shrinking the space. In fact, the space of the Cartesian product is also limited, because it is limited by the basic elements that constitute its own space. If you think about it carefully, this is quite similar to our world. To some extent, we are all composed of a limited number of elements. This also illustrates the universality of discreteness in our world. In addition, we need to talk about the meaning of binary relationships. Why are binary relationships so important? This question has to be addressed from the perspective of human cognition. Initially, the way humans understood things was simple, so how do people describe the world? Using one yuan, one yuan shows obvious isolation. It can depict elements, but it cannot depict the relationship between elements. So we need to add one element, thus forming a binary, which can not only reflect the elements, but also reflect the relationship between the elements. So the question is why not use triadic relationships or more triadic relationships as the basis? Because triadic relationships serve as the basis, then the level of binary relationships becomes unexpressible. However, binary relationships can be used to express ternary or even multi-dimensional relationships (this will be more reflected in pictures and trees). So you will find that many human sciences are based on the study of this binary relationship, because this relationship can be simple enough and consistent with human thinking. For example, Newton initially studied the relationship between two problems, and then hypothesized that this universal gravitation could be superimposed to some extent. Just study two things (elements), and then make some assumptions (and then depict the entire system on the macroscopic celestial body, so concisely). If you observe carefully, you will find that almost all operations can be decomposed into binary operations, and the role of operators is generally to calculate some relationship between two things. Almost all relationships can be broken down into binary relationships. In addition, in machine learning, the most basic way for computers to classify data is binary classification. Feature selection, perceptrons, and support vector machines are all based on this. For details, see machine learning. Then there are graphs, trees. Graphs and trees are an expansion of binary relationships, but they organize many binary relationships to build a large environment (similar to applying the rules between two planets to the entire galaxy) Figure, trees are so common. In real life, there are many discrete things, and there must be many relationships between things, so there will naturally be a graph structure. This is also the significance of discreteness to reality. Through the simplification of this diagram, it will be easier for us to understand things, discuss things, and use algorithms on this mathematical model. Graphs and trees, to a certain extent, simply complete the depiction of the world. To discover information in the world depicted by graphs and trees and find things meaningful to humans, what is needed is a certain algorithm, and the algorithm acts on the model (data structure). Computers also store things discretely. This similarity determines that computers can be used to deal with discrete problems encountered in life, or problems that can be transformed into discrete problems. So do you need to learn discrete mathematics to learn programming? Discrete mathematics may indeed be a patchwork of courses, just like Ruby. So a certain student in the mathematics department has never learned discrete mathematics, but has learned discrete content in several other courses. I once searched for discrete courses on Youtube, but only found two. One was taught by an Indian aunt, and the other was from a cram school run by an MIT alumni. This cram school closed after only one year. Since discrete mathematics is so necessary, why can’t we find formal classroom videos from foreign universities at all? According to teachers at MIT cram schools, most schools do not teach discrete mathematics because the content of discrete mathematics has been scattered in various computer courses. With the abundant resources of MIT OCW, I can only find one course, Mathematics for Computer Science. A small part of it is used to discuss discrete topics. So my conclusion is that it is not necessary to specialize in discrete mathematics. You can make up for whatever is missing. If you don’t understand something, just check the wiki and ask more questions to fill in the missing parts.
