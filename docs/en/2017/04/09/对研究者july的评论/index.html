You have to read original documents and papers in a down-to-earth manner.Comments on researcher July Author: Jason Copper Links:https://www.zhihu.com/question/24957182/answer/70481601Source: Zhihu Let me tell you my intuitive feelings: I knew him fairly well. I invited him to dinner and he invited me to drink coffee. I also reviewed his so-called svm manuscript before. I have nothing to say about his manuscript, which is basically pieced together and has no personal understanding. I reminded him of the revision suggestions before, but basically failed to do so. He was unwilling to change even the basic notations, so I later cut off contact with him. Until a while ago, I was invited to a lecture group to help me read the ppt of the lecture and give my opinions on modifications. I said a few words in the spirit of helping others, but of course some of my words were quite irritating (I really hate people who don’t read papers and are free to make their own imaginations). July was not nice and pointed at me personally, so I quarreled with them and got a little angry. In the end, I left the group and was too lazy to talk to them. Later, I reposted a few words out of fun and was scratched by him. Generally speaking, my evaluation of him is: he can stick to one thing and has perseverance, but he is too eager for quick success and has some behavior to deceive novices. One last word of advice to those newbies:There is no shortcut to doing research and doing academic work. You have to read the original documents and read the paper in a down-to-earth manner.. If prml or ESL is difficult first, then you can choose the blue book by teacher Li Hang; if you can't read the paper, you can go to arxiv to read some other people's notes. There is always a way to solve the problem. My previous introduction to machine learning was to get the prob and stat original book and prml from CMU. It was really difficult at the beginning. I had to watch it at 1 or 2 o'clock in the middle of the night every day, which was also a bit painful. But when you learn something, there is an indescribable joy. I have never stopped reading the paper since then.I still retain the habit of reading papers because in academic pursuits I have to constantly update my knowledge, otherwise I just sit back and watch the sky and let myself make random nonsense.。
