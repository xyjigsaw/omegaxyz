Overview of NSGA-II multi-objective genetic algorithm

技术域机器学习进化算法多目标优化算法NSGA-II
What is NSGA-II Non dominated sorting genetic algorithm -II NSGA-II is one of the most popular multi-objective genetic algorithms at present. It reduces the complexity of the non-inferior sorting genetic algorithm, has the advantages of fast running speed and good convergence of solution sets, and has become the benchmark for the performance of other multi-objective optimization algorithms. NSGA-Ⅱ is improved on the basis of the first generation of non-dominated sorting genetic algorithm. Its improvements are mainly aimed at the three aspects mentioned above: ① A fast non-dominated sorting algorithm is proposed. On the one hand, it reduces the computational complexity. On the other hand, it merges the parent population with the offspring population, allowing the next generation population to be selected from double the space, thus retaining all the best individuals; ②Introduce elite strategies to ensure that some excellent population individuals will not be discarded during the evolution process, thereby improving the accuracy of the optimization results; ③ The use of crowding degree and crowding degree comparison operator not only overcomes the shortcomings in NSGA that require artificially specified shared parameters, but also uses it as a comparison standard between individuals in the population, so that individuals in the quasi-Pareto domain can evenly expand to the entire Pareto domain, ensuring the diversity of the population. The purpose of the algorithm: select N individuals (M>N) from the current M individuals. NSGA-II key algorithm (steps) 1. First find pareto solutions for M individuals. Then get the set of F1, F2...etc. these pareto. 2. Put all the individuals of F1 into N. If N is not full, continue to put F2 until there are Fk that cannot be put into the N (space) that has already been put into F1, F2,...,F(k-1). At this time, Fk is solved. 3. For the individuals in Fk, find the crowding distance Lk[i] (crowding distance) of each individual in Fk, sort them in descending order according to Lk[i] in fk, and put them into N until N is full. NSGA-II key subroutine algorithm 1. Fast non-dominated sorting algorithm The key to multi-objective optimization problems is to find the Pareto optimal solution set. NSGA-II fast non-dominated sorting stratifies the population M according to the level of individual non-inferior solutions to obtain Fi. The effect is to make the solution close to the pareto optimal solution. This is a cyclic fitness value grading process. First, find the non-dominated solution set in the group, marked as F1, assign all its individuals to the non-dominated order irank=1 (where irank is the non-dominated order value of individual i), and remove it from the entire group M. Then continue to find the non-dominated solution set in the remaining group, marked as F2. The individuals in F2 are assigned irank=2. Continue in this way until the entire population is stratified and the non-dominated order values ​​in the Fi layer are the same. 2. Individual crowding distance Selective sorting is required in the same layer of Fk, sorting according to the individual crowding distance. The individual crowding distance is the distance between individuals i+1 and i-1 adjacent to i on Fk. Its calculation steps are: ① Initialize the distance between individuals in the same layer, let L[i]d=0 (representing the crowding distance of any individual i). ②Arrange the individuals in the same layer in ascending order according to the m-th objective function value. ③ Give selective advantages to individuals on the edge of sorting. ④For the individuals in the middle of the ranking, find the crowding distance:(Where: L[i+1]m is the m-th objective function value fmax of the i+1-th individual, and fmin are the maximum and minimum values of the m-th objective function in the set respectively.) ⑤ For different objective functions, repeat steps ② to ④ to obtain the crowding distance L[i]d of individual i. By limited selection of individuals with larger crowding distances, the calculation results can be evenly distributed in the target space and the diversity of the group can be maintained. 3. Elite strategy selection algorithm Keep the excellent individuals in the parent generation and directly enter the offspring to prevent the Pareto optimal solution from being lost. The selection index optimizes the population Ri synthesized by the parent Ci and the offspring Di to form a new parent Ci+1. First eliminate the solutions with unfeasible program test flags in the parent generation, and then put the entire population into Ci+1 from low to high according to the non-dominated order value irank, until Fk in a certain layer exceeds the limit of N. Finally, fill Ci+1 according to the crowding distance until the number of populations reaches N. Note: In multi-objective planning, due to conflicts and incomparability between goals, a solution may be the best for one goal, but may be worse for other goals. Pareto proposed the concept of multi-objective solutions (Non-dominated set) in 1986. Its definition is: assuming that any two solutions S1 and S2 are better than S2 for all goals, then we say S1 dominates S2. If the solution of S1 is not dominated by other solutions, then S1 is called a non-dominated solution (undominated solution), also called a Pareto solution. The set of these non-dominated solutions is the so-called Pareto Front. All solutions located in the Pareto front are not dominated by solutions outside the Pareto Front (and other solutions within the Pareto Front curve). Therefore, these non-dominated solutions have the least goal conflict compared with other solutions and can provide decision makers with a better choice space.
