Overview of Generalized Coevolutionary Algorithms

TechnologyMachine LearningEvolutionary Algorithms协同进化
Co-evolutionary algorithm is a new type of evolutionary algorithm proposed on the basis of co-evolution theory in the past ten years. The co-evolution algorithm takes into account the coordination between the population and the environment, and between the population and the population in the evolutionary process. Although research in this area started late, due to the superiority of co-evolutionary algorithms, more and more scholars have conducted research on it. At present, co-evolutionary algorithms have become an active research branch in the field of natural computing.Classification of coevolution: ①Co-evolution algorithm based on competition mechanism between populations:This type of algorithm consists of several populations, which are in a competitive relationship, but at the same time have cooperative behaviors. The purpose of information exchange is achieved through the migration of individuals among the populations.②Co-evolution algorithm based on predator-prey mechanism:The predator-prey relationship is a feedback mechanism between individuals that are subject to selective pressure. This feedback mechanism provides a powerful driver for the system to become more complex. In order to avoid being hunted, the prey develops certain means to better protect itself, but this also leads the predator to develop better attack strategies.③Co-evolution algorithm based on symbiotic mechanism:This type of algorithm decomposes the problem into several problems. Each sub-problem corresponds to a population, and each population is evolved using an evolutionary algorithm. Faced with a problem to be solved, each evolving individual only corresponds to a partial solution to the problem, and a symbiosis composed of individuals from different populations corresponds to a complete solution to the problem. They can acquire the chromosomal genes of one individual and infect another individual, causing corresponding changes in some of the chromosomal genes of that individual, thus changing the genetic information of that individual.
