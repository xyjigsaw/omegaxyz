TechnologyMachine LearningEvolutionary Algorithms
What is a heuristic algorithm?An algorithm based on intuition or experience can provide a feasible solution for each instance of the optimization problem to be solved at an acceptable cost (referring to computing time, occupied space, etc.). The deviation of the feasible solution from the optimal solution may not necessarily be predicted in advance. Heuristic algorithm is a technology that allows to find the best solution within an acceptable computational cost, but it does not necessarily guarantee the feasibility and optimality of the obtained solution. In most cases, it is even impossible to explain how close the obtained solution is to the optimal solution. Hyper-Heuristic Algorithm provides a high-level heuristic method by managing or manipulating a series of low-level heuristics (LLH) to generate new heuristic algorithms.Hyperheuristic algorithm vs. traditional heuristic algorithm: The figure above gives the conceptual model of the hyperheuristic algorithm. The model is divided into two levels: at the problem domain level, application domain experts provide a series of LLH and problem definitions, evaluation functions and other information based on their own background knowledge with the assistance of intelligent computing experts; at the high-level heuristic method level, intelligent computing experts design efficient management and control mechanisms and use the LLH algorithm library and problem characteristic information provided by the problem domain to construct new heuristic algorithms.
