The development history of databases and current mainstream technologies and products

TechnologyDatabasesDatabases

Chapter 1 Preface

Science and technology are the primary productive forces, and data are the foundation and source of scientific research. The emergence and development of database technology play a vital role in data storage, processing and information mining. This article will systematically introduce the development history of database technology, current mainstream technologies and products, and make reasonable predictions about the future development and evolution of databases.

1.1 Overview of database technology

Database is a warehouse that organizes, stores and manages data according to data structure. It was created fifty years ago. With the development of information technology and market, especially after the 1990s, data management is no longer just about storing and managing data, but has transformed into various data management methods required by users. There are many types of databases, ranging from the simplest tables that store various data to large database systems that can store massive amounts of data. They are widely used in various aspects.

1.2 The value of database technology

From the emergence of database technology to today's society, databases have played an indelible technology in data management and greatly promoted the development of scientific research. In the past few decades, database technology has evolved through three generations and has made very brilliant achievements: it has created three Turing Award winners: Bachman, Codd and Gray; it has developed a discipline with rich content based on core technologies of data modeling and database management system (DBMS); it has driven a huge software industry worth tens of billions of dollars. In the era of Internet big data and artificial intelligence, the control, screening and management of data reflect the degree of informatization of a country.

Chapter 2  The development history of database technology

Following technological advancements in areas such as processors, computer memory, computer storage, and computer networks, the size, performance, and performance of databases and their respective DBMSs have increased by orders of magnitude. The development of database technology can be divided into three eras based on data models or structures: the navigation database era, the SQL/relational era, and the post-relational era. Among them, post-relational includes the development of object-oriented databases and new database technologies. The following introduces the mainstream database technologies in each period in chronological order.

2.1 The origin of the database

More than 60 years ago, data was managed in a primitive and crude way. The data was processed through massive classification, comparison, and tabulation machines that ran millions of punched cards, and the results were printed on paper or made into new punched cards. Data management is the physical storage and processing of all these punched cards. But this does not meet the growing demand for data management control. It is inevitable that data is organized in some abstract way to make storage and retrieval more efficient.

2.2 In the 1960s, navigation-based database management systems

In the 1960s, computers began to be widely used in data management, but higher and higher requirements were placed on data sharing. Traditional file systems can no longer meet people's needs. Database management systems (DBMS) that can uniformly manage and share data emerged [1]. The data model is the core and foundation of the database system, and various DBMS software are based on a certain data model. Therefore, traditional database systems are usually divided into three categories: network database, hierarchical database and relational database according to the characteristics of the data model.

2.3 In the early 1970s, the development of relational databases

In 1970, Codd wrote a number of papers outlining new methods of database construction, culminating in the groundbreaking Linked Data model for large shared databases. Codd describes a new system for storing and processing large databases. The relational model works by breaking the data into a series of normalized tables, with optional elements moved out of the main table into locations that only take up space when needed. Data can be freely inserted, deleted and edited in these tables and the DBMS can do any maintenance to present the table view to the application/user. The relational model also allows the content of the database to evolve without having to constantly rewrite links and pointers. The relationship part comes from entities that reference other entities, called one-to-many relationships, like traditional hierarchical models, and many-to-many relationships like navigation (network) models [2], [3]. Thus, a relational model can express either a hierarchical model or a navigation model, as well as its native tabular model, allowing pure or combined modeling of these three models, depending on the needs of the application.

2.4 Development of SQL in the late 1970s

In the early 1970s, IBM began developing a prototype system, System R, loosely based on Codd's concepts. The first version was ready in May 1974, and work began on a multi-table system where data could be split so that all data recorded did not have to be stored in a single large "chunk". Subsequent multi-user versions were tested by customers in 1978 and 1979, at which time a standardized query language - SQL (Structured Query Language) - was added. Structured Query Language is a high-level, non-procedural programming language that allows users to work on high-level data structures. It does not require users to specify the data storage method, nor does it require users to understand the specific data storage method, so different database systems with completely different underlying structures can use the same structured query language as the interface for data input and management. Structured Query Language statements can be nested[4], which makes it extremely flexible and powerful.

2.5 1990s, object-oriented database

One of the first issues facing databases is the rise of non-character (alphanumeric) data. Increasing numbers of images, sound files, maps and videos need to be stored, manipulated and retrieved. This has resulted in a lot of innovation, but also fragmented standards.

Figure 3: Object-oriented database model

At this time, object-oriented database came into being. Object-oriented database system (OODBS) supports the definition and operation of OODB and should meet two criteria: firstly, it is a database system, and secondly, it is also an object-oriented system. The first standard is the capabilities that a database system should have (persistence, transaction management, concurrency control, recovery, query, version management, integrity, security). The second standard requires object-oriented databases to fully support complete object-oriented (OO) concepts and control mechanisms [5]. To sum up, we abbreviate object-oriented database as: object-oriented database = object-oriented system + database capabilities.

2.6 At the beginning of the 21st century, the development of NoSQL and NewSQL technologies

NoSQL generally refers to non-relational databases. With the rise of Internet web2.0 websites, traditional relational databases have been unable to cope with web2.0 websites, especially ultra-large-scale and highly concurrent SNS type web2.0 purely dynamic websites, exposing many insurmountable problems [7]. Non-relational databases have developed very rapidly due to their own characteristics. NoSQL databases were created to solve the challenges brought by large-scale data collections and multiple data types, especially big data application problems. NoSQL (NoSQL = Not Only SQL), which means "not just SQL", is a brand-new revolutionary database movement. It was proposed in the early days, and the trend became more and more intense in 2009. Advocates of NoSQL advocate the use of non-relational data storage [6]. Compared with the overwhelming application of relational databases, this concept is undoubtedly an injection of new thinking. The term NewSQL was proposed in a research paper by Matthew, an analyst at 451 Group. It refers to a new type of database system that challenges established database vendors. NewSQL is the abbreviation for various new scalable/high-performance databases. This type of database not only has the storage and management capabilities of NoSQL for massive data, but also maintains the characteristics of traditional databases such as ACID and SQL.

2.7 Further development of database technology

A data warehouse is a structured data environment for data sources for decision support systems and online analytical applications. Data warehousing studies and solves the problems of obtaining information from databases. Data warehouses are characterized by subject orientation, integration, stability and time variability. Data warehouse was proposed by Bill Enmen, the father of data warehouse, in 1990. Its main function is still to systematically analyze and organize the large amounts of data accumulated by organizations over the years through online transaction processing of information systems through the unique data storage architecture of data warehouse theory to facilitate various analysis. Analysis methods such as online analytical processing and data mining are carried out, and then support the creation of decision support systems and supervisory information systems, helping decision-makers to quickly and effectively analyze valuable information from large amounts of data to facilitate decision-making and rapid response to changes in the external environment, and help build business intelligence. Further developments in database technology will be highlighted in Chapter 3.

Chapter 3 Introduction and prospects of today’s mainstream database technologies and products

3.1 Mainstream database technology

3.1.1 Information integration

With the rapid development of the Internet, the network has quickly become an important means of information dissemination and exchange. Information integration system methods can be divided into: data warehouse method and Wrapper/Mediator method. In the data warehouse method, data from each data source is extracted and transformed from each data source according to the required global pattern, and stored in the data warehouse. The user's query is to query the data in the data warehouse. Another method is the Wrapper/Mediator method [8].

3.1.2 Sensor database technology

With the development of microelectronics technology, the applications of sensors are becoming more and more widespread. Based on the data sent back by the sensor within a certain range, useful information is collected within a certain range and sent back to the command center. When multiple sensors work within a certain range, a sensor network is formed. The sensor network consists of sensors bundled by the carrier and servers that receive and process data sent back by the sensors.

3.1.3 Mobile data management

More and more people own handheld or laptop computers, personal digital assistants or even smartphones. These mobile computers will be equipped with wireless networking devices. Users no longer need to be fixedly connected to a certain network, but can carry mobile computers and move around freely. This kind of computing environment is what we call mobile computing. Studying data management technology in mobile computing environment has become a new direction of current distributed database research, that is, mobile database technology.

3.1.4 Data encryption technology

We often need a measure to protect our data from being seen or destroyed by people with bad intentions. In the information age, information can help groups or individuals and benefit them. Similarly, information can also be used to threaten them and cause damage. In large, competitive companies, industrial spies often obtain intelligence from each other. Therefore, a strong security measure is objectively needed to protect confidential data from being stolen or tampered with.

3.2 Mainstream products of (relational) database

3.2.1 ORACLE

The ORACLE database system is a set of software products with a distributed database as the core provided by the American ORACLE company. It is currently one of the most popular client/server (CLIENT/SERVER) architecture databases. ORACLE database is currently the most widely used database management system in the world. As a general database system, it has complete data management functions; as a relational database, it is a complete relational product; as a distributed database, it implements distributed processing functions. It has the advantages of strong availability, strong scalability, strong data security, and strong stability.

3.2.2 SQL Server

SQL Server is a comprehensive database platform that provides enterprise-class data management using integrated business intelligence (BI) tools. The SQL Server database engine provides more secure and reliable storage capabilities for relational and structured data, allowing you to build and manage highly available and high-performance data applications for business. The SQL Server data engine is the core of many enterprise data management solutions.

3.2.3 MySQL

MySQL is an open source small relational database management system developed by the Swedish MySQL AB company. Currently, MySQL is widely used in small and medium-sized websites on the Internet. Due to its small size, fast speed, low total cost of ownership, and especially the characteristics of open source, many small and medium-sized websites choose MySQL as their website database in order to reduce the total cost of website ownership. Its shortcomings are mainly small scale, limited functions, and no support for views, events, etc.

3.2.4 DB2

A relational database system developed by IBM. DB2 is mainly used in large-scale application systems. It has good scalability and can support everything from mainframe to single-user environments. It is applied to OS/2, Windows and other platforms. DB2 provides a high level of data utilization, integrity, security, recoverability, and execution capabilities for small to large-scale applications, with platform-independent basic functions and SQL commands.

3.2.5 Informix

Informix is ​​a family of relational database management systems produced by IBM (2001). As an integrated solution, it is positioned as IBM's flagship online transaction processing data services system. Informix was established in 1980 to provide professional relational database products for open operating systems such as Unix. The company's name, Informix, is derived from the combination of Information and Unix. Informix's first relational database product that truly supports the SQL language is Informix SE. InformixSE was the main database product in the microcomputer Unix environment at that time. It was also the first commercial database product to be ported to Linux.

3.2.6 Sybase

A relational database system developed by the American Sybase Company, it is a typical large database system in a client/server environment on UNIX or Windows NT platforms. Sybase provides a set of application programming interfaces and libraries that can be integrated with non-Sybase data sources and servers, allowing data to be copied between multiple databases and suitable for creating multi-tier applications. The system has complete triggers, stored procedures, rules and integrity definitions, supports optimized queries, and has good data security.

3.3 Data technology development trends

3.3.1 Performance and ease of use are still the only way to improve the database

One of the main reasons for the slow upgrade of relational databases is that there are no key technological innovations. The main work done by major manufacturers is to continuously improve their products. This development direction is to enhance the database in a direction with fewer requirements. The so-called less demand means that the database runs with less relative resource consumption and higher performance. With the continuous advancement of technology, the database becomes smarter, and maintenance and use will be easier. This is the only way for database technology to improve in the process [9].

3.3.2 Search is the future of databases

With the continuous improvement of database technology, the continuous accumulation of user data, and the continuous improvement of user needs, more advanced applications have emerged, including mature data warehouse applications, widely accepted business intelligence applications, etc. When the database can accommodate almost all data, one problem we must face is how to quickly obtain the data we need. When users access the database, how to reduce the user's waiting and quickly return the data the user needs is a problem that all databases need to solve.

3.3.3 Open source databases are expected to become mainstream applications

Just as the Linux operating system is getting better and better, open source database systems are becoming mainstream in applications. Currently, the main open source database products include MySQL, MaxDB and PostgreSQL. After the MySQL 5.0 version upgrade, the MySQL database becomes more and more like large databases such as SQL Server, and gradually expands from the open source circle to the enterprise market. Open source database software is being recognized by more and more users for its low cost, forcing mainstream manufacturers to launch free versions in response.

Conclusion

The development of database technology is synchronized with the development of computer technology. From a simple database to the integration of related model libraries and knowledge bases, its achievements are remarkable. Of course, all of these still have many limitations, there are still many key issues to be solved, and database technology also faces new challenges. Efficiency, stability, and high security will be the common goals pursued by all database technologies. From the perspective of domestic database technology development, China's current research and application of database technology are still at a low level, and there is still a large gap with developed countries, which is worth noting. With the popularization of computers and social informatization, database technology will play an increasingly important role. In the future information society, database technology will surely develop greater and faster.
