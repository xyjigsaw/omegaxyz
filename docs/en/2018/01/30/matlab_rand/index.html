MATLAB random number generator

高级语言技术域MatlabMatlab
1、rand()
Generate random numbers uniformly distributed on the interval (0,1)
Basic syntax:rand([M,N,P...])
Generate random numbers arranged into M*N*P*... multidimensional vectors. If only M is written, an M*M matrix will be generated; if the parameters are [M, N], the square brackets can be omitted.
2、randn()
Generate random numbers obeying the standard normal distribution (mean 0, variance 1)
Basic syntax:randn([M,N,P,...])
The explanation is the same as 1
If the Statistic Toolbox is installed, in addition to the above two basic distributions, you can also use Matlab internal functions to generate random numbers that conform to the following distributions.
3、unifrnd()
Generate random numbers uniformly distributed within an interval
Basic syntax:unifrnd(a,b,[M,N,P,...])
The generated random numbers are arranged into M*N*P... multi-dimensional vectors within the interval (a, b). If only M is written, an M*M matrix will be generated; if the parameter is [M, N], the square brackets can be omitted.
4、normrnd()
Generates random numbers from a normal distribution with a specified mean and standard deviation.
Basic syntax:normrnd(mu,sigma,[M,N,P,...])
5、chi2rnd()
Generate random numbers obeying the chi-square distribution. The chi-square distribution has only one parameter: degree of freedom v
Basic syntax:chi2rnd(v,[M,N,P,...])
6、frnd()
Generate random numbers obeying F distribution. F distribution has two parameters: v1, v2
Basic syntax:frnd(v1,v2,[M,N,P,...])
7、trnd()
Generate random numbers that obey t (Student's t Distribution, here student does not mean student, but the pen name of cosset.W.S.) distribution. The t distribution has only one parameter: degree of freedom v
Basic syntax:trnd(v,[M,N,P,...])
The t distribution is "thinner" than the normal distribution. As the degree of freedom v increases, the t distribution gradually becomes fatter. When the degree of freedom becomes positive and infinite, it becomes the standard normal distribution.
8、betarnd()
Generate random numbers following beta distribution. The beta distribution has two parameters A and B. The figure below is the PDF graph of the beta distribution of A=2, B=5.
Basic syntax:betarnd(A,B,[M,N,P,...])
9、exprnd()
This function generates random numbers following an exponential distribution. The exponential distribution has only one parameter: mu. The following figure is the PDF graphic of the exponential distribution when mu=3.
Basic syntax:exprnd(mu,[M,N,P,...])
10、gamrnd()
Generate random numbers obeying gamma distribution. The Gamma distribution has two parameters: A and B. The picture below is the PDF graphic of A=2, B=5, Gamma distribution
Basic syntax:gamrnd(A,B,[M,N,P,...])
11、logrnd()
Generate random numbers following a lognormal distribution. It has two parameters: mu and sigma. After taking the logarithm of the random numbers obeying this distribution, they all obey the normal distribution with mean mu and standard deviation sigma. The picture below is the PDF graph of the lognormal distribution with mu=1, sigma=1/1.2.
Basic syntax:logrnd(mu,sigma,[M,N,P,...])
12、raylrnd()
Generate random numbers following the Rayleigh distribution. Its distribution has 1 parameter: B. The figure below is the PDF graphic of the Rayleigh distribution of B=2
Basic syntax:raylrnd(B,[M,N,P,...])
13、wblrnd()
Generate random numbers following Weibull distribution. Its distribution has two parameters: scale parameter A and shape parameter B. The picture below is the PDF graph of the Weibull distribution with A=3 and B=2.
Basic syntax:wblrnd(A,B,[M,N,P,...])
 
There is also a non-central chi-square distribution (ncx2rnd), non-central F distribution (ncfrnd), non-central t distribution (nctrnd)
 
14、unidrnd()
Generate random numbers following a discrete uniform distribution. unifrnd is to uniformly select real numbers (can be decimals or integers) within a certain interval, and unidrnd is to uniformly select integer random numbers. The discrete uniformly distributed random number has 1 parameter: n, which means from {1,2,3,. . . , n} These n integers are sampled with the same probability.
Basic syntax:unidrnd(n,[M,N,P,...])
15、binornd()
This function generates random numbers following a binomial distribution. The binomial distribution has 2 parameters: n, p
Basic syntax:binornd(n,p,[M,N,P,...])
16、geornd()
Generate random numbers following a geometric distribution. The geometric distribution has only one parameter p
Basic syntax:geornd(p,[M,N,P,...])
17、poissrnd()
Generate random numbers obeying the possion distribution. There is only one parameter of position distribution: lambda. This parameter must be greater than zero.
Basic syntax:poissrnd(lambda,[M,N,P,...])
