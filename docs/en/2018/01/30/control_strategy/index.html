6 Common Manipulation Tactics in Relationships

IdeasReposts随笔Ideas
Why do people manipulate? Braiker (2003) believes that manipulative behavior in relationships is mainly motivated by three motives. Although in many cases, manipulators may not be aware of their own motives, they are just manipulating instinctively.1. Achieve your own goals:Manipulators want to achieve some goal, such as gaining money, status, sex, love, winning, or avoiding loss, and they believe that manipulation is a shortcut to achieving their goals.2. Gain a sense of power and advantage:A successful manipulation is confirmation and proof that the manipulator has a sense of power in the relationship.3. Satisfy the need for a sense of control:Manipulators fear losing control, and sometimes, having a sense of control is more important to a manipulator than actually having control.
Starting from these three motivations, common ones in relationships include6 Manipulation Strategies(Braiker, 2003)： 1. to please(Charming): Acting charming in front of the other person; praising the other person; trying to be loving and romantic when making requests; carefully preparing gifts/surprises when meeting; letting him/her know that if he does what I want, I will give him some benefits. For example, a person who uses pleasing as a weapon of manipulation might say, "I do this because I love/care about you."2. Cold War(thesilent treatment): Unless the other person does what I want, otherwise I will adopt an attitude of not responding/ignoring/remaining silent/doing nothing.3. Force(coercion): Criticizing/yelling/threats (such as suicide, self-harm, etc.) unless the other person does what I want them to do.The essence of forced behavior is to emotionally kidnap the other person. There is a common method of emotional kidnapping in relationships called guilt induction, which is to make the other person feel guilty about themselves and then manipulate them to get the results they want. For example, crying and saying, "I am so lonely at home, but you are always going out to work."4. Persuasion(reasoning): Listing various reasons, such as all possible good results, in order to persuade the other party to do something; repeatedly asking the other party why they do not do it; explaining why you want the other party to do it. Sometimes manipulators willPretending to be an expert: "I'm telling you this because I have more experience in these things and I know better than you." Or toConvince the other person and tell them that you are willing to do this for them:"I will do these things for you, even if it does me no good."5. Regression（regression）：Regression is a defense mechanism that refers to a person's conscious or unconscious behavior that is childish and inappropriate for his or her age.For example, speaking in a childish tone or acting coquettishly in order to get the other person to do what you want; unless the other person does this, you will appear unhappy/sulky.6. Self-deprecation(debasement): In order to get the other person to do something, the manipulator can belittle himself, behave in a groveling manner, or allow himself to be humiliated, such as kneeling down, slapping himself, hurting himself, etc.
