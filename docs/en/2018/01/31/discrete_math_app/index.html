Applications of Discrete Mathematics in Computer Science

Technology其它离散数学
Since our college implemented software engineering certification, all professional courses in the final exam are big questions. The last question on discrete mathematics this time is: use the knowledge of discrete mathematics learned this semester to explain its application in a software engineering. Let’s talk about the applications of discrete mathematics.

Application of discrete mathematics in data structures

In the data structure, the relationships between operating objects are divided into four categories: sets, linear structures, tree structures, graph structures or network structures. The main content of data structure research is the logical structure of data, physical storage structure and basic operations. The logical structure and basic operations are derived from the discrete structure and algorithmic thinking in discrete mathematics. The four chapters of set theory, relations, graph theory, and trees in discrete mathematics reflect the knowledge of the four major structures in data structures. If a set is composed of elements, elements can be understood as objective things in the world. A relationship is a relationship between the elements of a set. For example, the relationship between employees and their wages. Graph theory is an ancient topic with many modern applications. Swiss mathematician Leonhard Euler introduced the basic ideas of graph theory in the 18th century. He used graphs to solve the Königsberg Seven Bridges Problem. You can also use graphs with weighted edges to solve problems such as finding the shortest path between two cities in a transportation network. The tree reflects the relationship between objects, such as organizational charts, family diagrams, and binary coding, all discussed using trees as models.

Application of discrete mathematics in databases

Database technology is widely used in various fields of society, and relational databases have become the mainstream of databases. The Cartesian product in discrete mathematics is a pure mathematical theory and an important method for studying relational databases, showing an irreplaceable role. It not only provides theoretical and methodological support, but more importantly, promotes the research and development of database technology. The relational data model is based on strict set algebra. The logical structure of the data is a two-dimensional table composed of rows and columns to describe the relational data model. Binary relationship theory is used in the study of possible relationships between domains in an entity set, the determination and design of table structures, the implementation of data query and maintenance functions for relationship operations, lossless connectivity analysis of relationship decomposition, and connection dependencies.

Application of discrete mathematics in compilation principles

The compiler is a very complex system program of the computer. A typical compiler generally contains eight parts: lexical analysis program, syntax analysis program, semantic analysis program, intermediate code generation program, code optimization program, target code generation program, error checking and processing program, and various information table management programs. The chapter on computational models in discrete mathematics discusses three types of computational models: grammars, finite state machines and Turing machines. Specific knowledge includes language and grammar, finite state machines with output, finite state machines without output, language recognition, Turing machines, etc. Phrase structure grammars are classified according to production types: Type 0 grammar, Type 1 grammar, Type 2 grammar, and Type 3 grammar. The above knowledge points described in discrete mathematics will be used in lexical analysis and syntax analysis of compilation principles. Therefore, discrete mathematics is also an early basic course on compilation principles.

Application of discrete mathematics in artificial intelligence

In the field of artificial intelligence research and application, logical reasoning is one of the most persistent subfields of artificial intelligence research. Logic is the basis of all mathematical reasoning and has practical applications for artificial intelligence. Formalization of the deductive process in the language of predicate logic helps us understand more clearly certain subpropositions of reasoning. Logic rules give precise definitions of mathematical statements. The knowledge in the chapters on mathematical reasoning and Boolean algebra in discrete mathematics laid a good mathematical foundation for the early field of artificial intelligence research. Many informal tasks, including medical diagnosis and information retrieval, can be formalized in the same way as theorem proving problems. Therefore, theorem proving is an extremely important topic in the study of artificial intelligence methods. Here, the inference engine is a program that implements (machine) reasoning. It includes both usual logical reasoning and production-based operations. The inference engine uses the knowledge in the knowledge base to reason and solve problems. Therefore, the inference engine is the thinking mechanism of experts, that is, an algorithmic representation and machine implementation of experts' methods of analyzing and solving problems. Of course, the above is an expert system. Generally speaking, this kind of artificial intelligence has been replaced by machine learning based on statistical learning.

Application of discrete mathematics in computer architecture

In the computer architecture, the design and improvement of the instruction system play a very important role. The optimization of the instruction system means the improvement of the performance of the entire computer system. There are many methods to optimize the instruction system. One method is to optimize the format of the instruction. A machine instruction is composed of an operation code and an address code. Optimization of the instruction format refers to how to use the shortest number of digits to represent the operation information and address information of the instruction, so that the average word length of the instructions in the program is the shortest. For this purpose, Huffman compression concept can be used. Huffman compression is a lossless compression method. The basic idea of ​​the Huffman compression concept is that when the probabilities of occurrence of various events are unequal, optimization techniques are used to represent (process) the events with the highest probability of occurrence with the shortest number of digits (time), while allowing longer number of digits (time) to represent (process) the events with lower probability of occurrence, which will lead to a shortening of the average number of digits (time) for representation (processing). Use the Huffman algorithm to construct a Huffman tree. The method is to count the frequency of use of all instructions in the instruction system and sort them from small to large. Each time, the two smallest frequencies are selected and merged into a new node whose frequency is the sum of the two. Then according to the frequency size, insert the remaining frequency values ​​that are not involved in the combination. This continues until all frequencies are combined to form the root node. After that, the two branches extending downward from each node are marked with "1" or "0" respectively. Starting from the root node, the code sequence along the line to reach each frequency node constitutes the Huffman code of the instruction. The coding series obtained in this way is in line with the original intention of encoding long codes for instructions with low instruction usage probability, and encoding short codes for instructions with high instruction usage probability.

Replenish

Discrete mathematics plays an increasing role in computer research. Some basic concepts, basic ideas, and basic methods in discrete mathematics are commonly used in computer science, making computer science more complete and mature. Discrete mathematics is widely used in computer science and technology. In addition to playing an important role in the fields mentioned above, it also has important applications in other fields. For example, the mathematical logic part of discrete mathematics is particularly prominent in computer hardware design. Digital logic, as an important theory of computer science, originated to a large extent from the propositions and logical calculations in the mathematical logic of discrete mathematics. Using the operation rules of each associated word in the proposition to connect the operations between the signals represented by high and low levels with the operations between binary numbers, we can use mathematical methods to solve circuit design problems, making the entire design process more intuitive and systematic. Set theory is also widely used in computer science. It lays a mathematical foundation for data structure and algorithm analysis. It also provides some important methods of abstraction and description for how to solve many problems from an algorithmic perspective. It is also used in software engineering and databases. Algebraic structure is the knowledge about operations or calculation rules. In computer science, algebraic methods are widely used in many sub-disciplines, such as computability and computational complexity, formal languages and automata, cryptography, network and communication theory, program theory and formal semantics, etc. Lattice and Boolean algebra theory have become the basis for the design and generalization of electronic computer hardware. Graph theory is an important tool in the design of communication systems. Graph theory plays an important role in switch theory and logic design, computer graphics, operating systems, programming language compilation systems, and information organization and retrieval. The practical value of its research on floor plans and trees is obvious for the wiring of integrated circuits, the laying of network lines, and the analysis of network information flow.
