How to charge lithium battery correctly

Ideas常识Ideas
I just replaced my mobile phone with a new battery and found that many people have misunderstandings about charging mobile phones and computers, such as the following rumor.gossip: A newly purchased mobile phone must be fully charged for more than 8 hours and repeated several times to fully charge and discharge. This process is called activation, so that the battery of the mobile phone can reach its maximum capacity. The phone must be unplugged immediately after it is fully charged, as overcharging can cause the battery to explode. It is best to use up the batteries of laptops and mobile phones before recharging them. This can minimize the number of charging times and extend battery life. the truth1. Does a new battery need to be fully charged for 12 hours? The first charge does not need to be fully charged for 12 hours. This is what old-fashioned nickel-metal hydride batteries need to do. Most of the batteries used in smartphones and laptops today are lithium-ion batteries or polymer lithium batteries. The first time you only need to charge as usual, the battery is full and the indicator light is on. 2. Can reducing charging times extend battery life? Lithium batteries have no memory effect and can be charged at any time. Deliberately using up the battery and then recharging it to full capacity in order to reduce the number of charges will not extend the battery life, but will have a negative impact on the battery life. In fact, when charging lithium batteries, it is important to "eat less and eat more". Frequent shallow charging and discharging will help extend its life. 3. Will the battery explode if you make a phone call while charging? Lithium batteries generally have safety protection circuits and various safety devices to ensure that the battery circuit is automatically cut off when overcharged, discharged or short-circuited. Therefore, unless there is a quality problem, the battery will not explode if it is plugged in and charged for a long time. However, from a safety perspective, do not cover the battery with anything during charging, and do not place it on the bed to avoid a fire. In addition, try not to answer calls while charging, because the electromagnetic radiation of a charging mobile phone will be relatively large. 4. Wait until the battery runs out before charging? Try to use up the battery of your mobile phone as much as possible, and it is best to use automatic shutdown. This method only applies to nickel batteries to avoid memory effects. Most mobile phones on the market now use lithium batteries, and the number of times they can be charged is closely related to the depth of discharge of the battery. Lithium batteries lose a lot when they are low on power, so waiting until they run out of power before charging will speed up their wear and tear. Keeping the battery at 40%~60% for a long time can make the battery last longer. 5. Will charging the phone overnight cause damage? When the mobile phone is fully charged and has not yet been unplugged, the mobile phone is powered directly by the power source. This reduces the number of battery charges and is beneficial to extending battery life. But if you always play games with the power supply on, the phone may overheat due to the high heat generated by the power supply, which will have an impact on the battery. Therefore, as long as it does not overheat, charging the phone overnight will not damage the battery. 6. The phone does not need to be shut down or restarted Should mobile phones be turned off like computers? Apple recommends that users shut down their phones once a week to extend battery life.
