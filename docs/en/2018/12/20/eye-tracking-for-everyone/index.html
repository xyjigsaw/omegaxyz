Introduction to eye tracking technology based on mobile devices and CNN

TechnologyMachine LearningDeep Learning计算机视觉神经网络CNN计算机视觉
Eye tracking is a scientific application technology that allows users to send information and receive feedback without physical contact with interactive devices. In principle, eye tracking mainly studies the acquisition, modeling and simulation of eye movement information, and has a wide range of uses. In addition to infrared devices, the devices that obtain eye movement information can also be image acquisition devices, or even cameras on ordinary computers or mobile phones, which can also achieve eye tracking with the support of software. The paper mentioned below is a paper published by MIT on CVPR in 2016. The paper is mainly based on eye tracking technology on mobile devices, using convolutional neural networks to predict viewpoints.Using eye-tracking technology on mobile devices offers many benefits:
  • (1) Smartphones are widely used by more than one-third of the world’s population as of 2019, far exceeding the number of desktop or laptop users;
  • (2) High adoption rate of technology upgrades - a large proportion of the population owns the latest hardware and can use computationally expensive methods such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in real time;
  • (3) The extensive use of cameras on mobile devices has led to the rapid development and deployment of camera technology;
  • (4) The fixed position of the camera relative to the screen reduces the number of unknown parameters.
The author developed an iOS software for collecting severe winter tracking data on mobile devices. It can record and upload data. It is called GazeCapture and has three features:
  • (1) Expandable
  • (2) Reliable
  • (3) Generate large variability.
Among them, variability: In order to learn robust eye tracking models, significant variation in the data is important. This variability is critical to achieving high-precision calibration-free eye tracking. Given that we use a crowdsourcing platform, expect a lot of variation in pose, appearance, and lighting. Second, participants were asked to constantly move their heads and the distance between their heads and the phone. Finally, participants were asked to change the orientation of their mobile device 60 points each time. This change can be detected on the device using built-in sensors. This changes the relative position of the camera and screen providing further variability. In order to associate the AMT tasks (mobile data activity) in each mobile device, each worker then entered the unique code in the AMT into their mobile application. The point positions are randomly derived from 13 fixed locations (loc), and the influence of calibration is learned through the fixed loc. In order to prove the variability of the data, the author used the method of X. Zhang, Y. Sugano, M. Fritz, and A. Bulling. Appearance-based gaze estimation in the wild to estimate the head pose h and gaze direction g for each frame, and plot the distribution of h and g on GazeCapture MPIIGaze and TabletGaze. Although our dataset contains a similar overall distribution h compared to existing datasets, the proportion of outliers is significantly larger.The goal of the paper is to design a robust model that can predict viewpoints from a picture, using CNN. The input includes original frame image detection of left eye, right eye, and face. The output is the distance from the camera in centimeters.In order to estimate the generalization ability of the model (via calibration-free), the following conclusions are drawn: unconstrained eye tracking, and ablation studies.
  • error: average Euclidean distance error;
  • dots error: frame average prediction error;
Paper: Krafka K, Khosla A, Kellnhofer P, et al. Eye Tracking for Everyone[C]// Computer Vision & Pattern Recognition. 2016.
