Edge Computing and Embedded Systems

  1. Introduction to edge computing

Edge computing is a method of processing data physically close to the location where the data is generated, that is, in live areas such as homes and remote offices where things and people are located. An open platform that integrates the core capabilities of network, computing, storage, and applications to provide edge intelligence services nearby to meet the key needs of industry digitalization in terms of agile connections, real-time services, data optimization, application intelligence, security, and privacy protection. Edge computing decomposes large services that are originally completely processed by central nodes [4], cuts them into smaller and more manageable parts, and distributes them to edge nodes for processing. Edge nodes are closer to user terminal devices, which can speed up data processing and transmission and reduce delays.

            Figure 1: Edge computing model

  1. The origins of edge computing

The big data processing stage with the cloud computing model as the core is called the centralized big data processing era [1]. The main characteristics of this stage are that the calculation and storage of big data are performed in the cloud computing center. The data center is executed in a centralized manner because the cloud computing center has strong computing and storage capabilities. This resource-concentrated big data processing method can save users a lot of expenses and create effective economies of scale. However, the centralized processing model of the cloud computing center shows its inherent problems in the era of the Internet of Everything, as shown in the following table:

Table 1: Main issues with cloud computing

1 The linear growth of centralized cloud computing capabilities cannot match the explosive growth of massive edge data.
2 The massive amount of data transmitted from network edge devices to cloud data centers increases the load on transmission bandwidth, resulting in long network latency.
3 Issues involving personal privacy and security of edge device data have become particularly prominent.
4 Edge devices have limited power, and data transmission causes terminal devices to consume large amounts of power.
This is where edge computing comes into play. Different from cloud computing, in edge computing, data no longer needs to be transmitted to a distant cloud, but can be solved at the edge. It is more suitable for real-time data analysis and intelligent processing, and is more efficient and secure. In the massive data, as business users and consumers have increasingly higher requirements for efficiency and speed, low latency has become the standard. In order to meet this demand, a new computing method: edge computing has entered the public eye. “Edge computing puts the right data in the right place at the right time, enabling fast and secure access.

  1. Embedded Systems and Edge Computing

 

3.1 Overview of embedded systems

Embedded system is application-centered, based on computer technology, and the software and hardware can be tailored. It is suitable for special computer systems where application systems have strict requirements on function, reliability, cost, volume, and power consumption.

Figure 2: Basic components of an embedded system

 

3.2 Development History of Embedded Systems

Since the advent of embedded systems in the 1970s, it has gone through several major development stages from microcontrollers to embedded CPUs to embedded operating systems. Entering the 21st century, embedded systems are undergoing informatization, intelligence, and network development to adapt to increasingly changing needs. The market for embedded systems is huge, and applications of embedded systems are almost everywhere: mobile communications, digital offices, home appliance applications, transportation, interactive entertainment, etc. It is everywhere. Now with the rise of artificial intelligence, the demand for data processing has risen to a higher level. Artificial intelligence has gradually migrated from the cloud to the embedded side, and embedded systems based on edge computing environments have emerged. (Such as Raspberry Pi, processors with edge computing capabilities, cameras based on edge computing, etc.)

3.3 Embedded systems applied to edge computing

Embedded hardware in an edge computing environment requires modules with edge computing functions as co-processing units, referred to as edge computing hardware units. Then the edge computing hardware unit needs to be integrated into the original embedded system hardware platform with corresponding embedded software support technology to realize an embedded system with edge computing capabilities. 3.5 Embedded systems and artificial intelligence in edge computing environment

Today, the world is entering a new era of digitalization. Digital technologies represented by cloud computing, big data, artificial intelligence (AI) and the Internet of Things are developing in full swing. By 2019, about 40% of raw data will need to be processed at the edge, and today there are few security protections. Artificial intelligence is now migrating to embedded systems. For example, Apple's A11 Bionic artificial intelligence processor and Amazon's smart speaker Echo, these new technologies have powerful edge computing capabilities. Handheld artificial intelligence and edge computing artificial intelligence led by Google and Apple have become industry trends. This is embedded artificial intelligence. The biggest feature of so-called embedded artificial intelligence is the localization of artificial intelligence, which can get rid of the constraints of the network. Embedded smart transportation based on edge computing: Intelligent traffic control systems [1] analyze data collected by surveillance cameras and sensors in real time and make decisions automatically. As the amount of traffic data increases, users' demand for real-time traffic information is also increasing. Transmitting this data to the cloud computing center will cause bandwidth waste and delays, and it will not be able to optimize services based on location recognition. An intelligent traffic control system is run on the edge server to analyze data in real time and use intelligent traffic lights to reduce vehicle congestion on the road or change driving routes based on the actual conditions of the road.

  1. views and opinions

4.1 Edge data security

Embedded systems in today's edge environments often handle sensitive information such as application code (IP) and large amounts of data, so security is a major concern in their design. Therefore, retaining encrypted edge data on embedded systems and allowing users to own this data is a better solution to protect private data [4]. For data collected by devices at the edge of the network, the data should be selectively deleted after edge computing. In addition, adding security measures such as specific encryption algorithms and security protocols to embedded systems is also a good choice.

4.2 Processing capabilities of artificial intelligence and embedded systems

Many artificial intelligence technologies rely on deep learning, and to process data accurately, the neural network built by deep learning must have many layers. Based on this understanding, Google launched a tensor processing unit (TPU) similar to a GPU, but with better tensor processing capabilities than a GPU. Similarly, when artificial intelligence is integrated into edge computing, the computing capabilities of its supporting embedded systems must also be improved. Similar to the A11 processor equipped with the iPhone X, this problem can be solved well. However, in a few years, the data processed may increase exponentially, which will be a challenge for future embedded systems. Of course, if the edge server is used to calculate the data of the edge node, it needs good short-range information communication capabilities, which also puts forward requirements for the equipment configuration of the edge node.

4.3 Mobile edge computing

Personally, I believe that the edge computing capabilities of embedded systems such as smartphones can improve 5G user experience and enhance augmented reality (AR) application scenarios in the future. [2][3] In addition to the 5G application requirements driven by high bandwidth and low latency, the content awareness and network capability opening capabilities of embedded systems are closely related to edge computing.

4.4 Social value of embedded edge computing

In the sixth episode of "Big Power 2", "Win on the Internet", it was introduced that China's Hangzhou was the first to use China Skynet's face recognition technology. It used tens of thousands of cameras to authenticate the detected facial images through the face database retrieval system, and compared them in sequence. In just 4 seconds, a lost elderly man was found. The lost old man in "Great Powers 2" was found within a 5KM area 4 hours after leaving home. There are 100,000 people within the 5KM area, involving thousands of high-definition cameras. Taking an ordinary 1 million pixel high-definition camera as an example, it will generate 1G of data every hour, 1,000 cameras will generate 1T of traffic per hour, and 4T of traffic will be generated in 4 hours. Imagine searching for one person among 100,000 people with a data volume of 4T, and identifying each person's face one by one in each frame. This amount of calculation will take a lot of time to process even with cloud computing. The use of edge computing, which enhances the computing and processing capabilities of camera terminals, allows the face recognition function to no longer rely on cloud servers, avoids time-consuming uploading of images, and saves a lot of bandwidth resources. And by completing facial recognition directly on the local device, the recognition process is shortened to 1.5 seconds. Through such an example, we can see that integrating embedded systems and edge computing technology into social life will greatly improve social efficiency, which will be beneficial to promoting the process of realizing socialist modernization in our country.
