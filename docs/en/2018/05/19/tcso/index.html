PSO algorithm (CSO) based on triple competition mechanism

TechnologyMachine LearningEvolutionary AlgorithmsPSO算法

TCSO algorithm source

Title of paper:A modiﬁed competitive swarm optimizer for large scale optimization problems author:Prabhujit Mohapatra , Kedar Nath Das, Santanu Roy Department of Mathematics, National Institute of Technology Silchar, Silchar, Assam, India Journal:Applied Soft Computing 30 May 2017

TCSO algorithm idea

① Assume that in the D-dimensional search space, the group size is N, and each particle i (1≤i≤N) in the group has the following two attributes
  • Position (p_i) ⃗(t)=(p_i1, p_i1,…,p_id,…,p_iD)
  • Flight speed (v_i) ⃗(t)=(v_i1, v_i1,…, v_id,…, v_iD)
②Next, during the iteration process, first calculate the fitness value (error rate) of each particle ③ During iteration, a competitive selection mechanism different from the simple single-objective CSO is used: 3 particles are randomly selected for competitive selection, the winner directly enters the descendant, and the two losers learn from the winner and enter the descendant after updating.

flow chart

Step1: Initialization Step2: Competition selection (Tri-competition) Step3: Particle upgrade (Upgradation)

TCSO algorithm analysis

①The single-target CSO with triple competition mechanism inherits the advantages of single-target CSO with dual competition mechanism. (Such as preventing PSO from falling into local optimality, etc.)②In the single-objective CSO with triple competition mechanism, 2/3 of the particles need to be updated in each iteration, which speeds up the convergence and leads to a better solution.③In the single-objective CSO with triple competition mechanism, 1/3 of the particles in each iteration directly enter the next generation, and 2/3 of the particles learn the winner, which improves the diversity of particles.④The time efficiency is the same as double CSO, which is O(mn).
