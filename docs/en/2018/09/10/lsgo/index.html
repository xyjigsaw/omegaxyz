Technologies and bottlenecks of high-dimensional (multivariable) optimization problems

TechnologyMachine LearningEvolutionary Algorithms特征选择
Optimization problems in the real world often have high complexity and dimensionality, and are called LSGO problems, or Large-Scale Global Optimization. This problem has attracted great interest in research efforts in various fields. Many scientific and engineering applications are formulated as LSGO problems, such as designing large electronic systems, scheduling problems of large amounts of resources, vehicles in large-scale traffic, routing problems in networks, gene identification in bioinformatics, inverse problems, chemical kinetics, etc. Over the past decade, a large number of metaheuristics have been developed or improved to significantly improve the performance of algorithms dealing with the LSGO problem.   • (1) The search space grows exponentially with the number of decision variables;
  • (2) Metaheuristic algorithms usually require too much computational cost (many dimensions and long time);
  • (3) The CC method based on the divide and conquer idea needs to study the interaction (correlation) of variables;
  • (4) The redundancy and irrelevance of high-dimensional features will reduce the efficiency and performance of the algorithm.
The above problems cause most algorithms for solving large-scale problems to perform poorly on data exceeding 1000D.References:Mahdavi S, Shiri M E, Rahnamayan S. Metaheuristics in large-scale global continues optimization: A survey[J]. Information Sciences, 2015, 295: 407-428.
