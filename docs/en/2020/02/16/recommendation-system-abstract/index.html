Recommendation system summary

TechnologyMachine LearningNatural Language ProcessingNatural Language Processing
As a layman, or user, of a recommendation system, I think the recommendation system has the following characteristics.
  • The real purpose of the recommendation system is not to satisfy users, but to improve sales capabilities, business levels and profits.
  • A good recommendation system does not recommend the things that users like/want the most. It is still difficult in industry and academia to quantitatively judge the quality of a recommendation system.
  • If the same recommendation algorithm is applied to different systems, the results will inevitably be different.
  • Simple and easy-to-understand recommendation algorithms can often achieve unexpected results.
The paper recommendation system is very similar to ordinary recommendation at first glance, but in fact there are big differences. 1. First of all, academic papers are very professional, and it is impossible to recommend a paper on electromagnetic interference technology to a medical researcher; 2. On the other hand, interdisciplinary research is a trend in academic research and a key point in academic innovation. A recommendation system has reasons to recommend scientific research work in materials science, chemistry, biology or computers to the above-mentioned medical researchers, but this cannot be regarded as the mainstream of recommendation; 3. At the same time, we can assume that this medical student has a great interest in astronomy in his spare time, so why not recommend a few papers on space exploration for this medical student to "fish"? In addition to the above points, there are many unexpected complications, which require an academic recommendation system to have strong adaptability. To be updated ---
