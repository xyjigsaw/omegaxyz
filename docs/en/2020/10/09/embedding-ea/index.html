Embedding-based entity alignment look-ahead

TechnologyMachine LearningKnowledge GraphsNatural Language Processing神经网络Natural Language Processing社交网络Knowledge Graphs
Excerpts from papers：Sun, Zequn, et al. "A Benchmarking Study of Embedding-based Entity Alignment for Knowledge Graphs." arXiv preprint arXiv:2003.07743 (2020).

Unsupervised entity alignment Unsupervised entity alignment

Most current methods require seed alignment as supervision. Therefore, studying unsupervised entity alignment is a meaningful direction. One possible solution is to incorporate auxiliary features or resources and perform remote supervision from them, such as discriminating features (personnel homepages and product introduction pictures) and pre-training. In addition, recent advances in unsupervised cross-language word alignment such as adversarial training are also worthy of study. Another possible solution is to use active learning to reduce the burden of data labeling.

Long-tail entity alignment Long-tail entity alignment

For the embedding of long-tail entities, in addition to using more advanced graph neural networks, injecting more features (such as multi-modal data and taxonomies) will also help. Since KG is not yet complete, jointly training link prediction and entity alignment through a unified framework may simultaneously exploit the supervision of both tasks. Extracting more information from open networks to enrich long-tail entities is also a potential direction.

Large-scale entity alignment Large-scale entity alignment

As the number of entities increases, the cost will grow exponentially. Since the candidate space is large and has no partitioning, it is difficult to run embedding-based methods on very large KGs.
