Orwell: Big Brother is Watching You - Ten Commandments for Software Companies

TechnologyIdeas随笔Developer LifeSoftware EngineeringIdeasSoftware Engineering
Orwell: Keeping an Eye On You Orwell: Keeping an Eye On You is a dystopian government surveillance simulator.The Orwell of them all is a new government security program with the power to investigate the online presence of everyone in "the nation." It can monitor all personal communications and access any computer. To protect citizens' privacy, human researchers examined the data Orwell discovered and decided which information should be passed on to security forces and which should be withheld. The Ten Commandments mentioned by Rosen Software are very interesting. The full text is as follows:

Ten Commandments

Rosen Technology believes that excellent quality and performance are inseparable from clear rules. Therefore, we must use the Ten Commandments to guide our work every day:

1. All visions have value.

Have you ever thought about how the future should be like this or that? How a certain problem should be solved? Of course you have. Don't waste these ideas. They are as precious as pure gold. Be bold and pursue what is in your heart.

2. Software can solve all problems.

That's right. We live in an age where information is everything. We can reduce vast amounts of complex facts into bits and bytes. We run carefully designed algorithms and marvel at the results when the problem is solved.

3. Embrace the customer’s vision.

We believe that our valued customers know their core needs best. Therefore, one should strive to achieve good design by following their vision.

4. Open-mindedness and transparency are crucial.

This principle is closely related to the third principle, and the importance of being open-minded and transparent in all processes cannot be underestimated. Don't neglect it, don't resist it. Don't be vague. It's that simple.

5. Always stay in touch with your customers.

Whoever said "ignorance is power" was dead wrong. There is no power if you don’t understand your customers’ needs. Even if there are 2 or 3 times more questions, ask them boldly.

6. Synchronous communication is better than asynchronous communication.

Don’t waste your client’s and your own time with asynchronous communications like email. Contacting people directly is more efficient and more approachable, and it also gives you the opportunity to discuss things directly and get feedback. So we didn't set up an email address at all.

7. Think about your responsibilities.

Our work not only means something to our clients and ourselves, but quite literally changes the world. You all need to take this to heart. Our power is so great that we should not take it lightly.

8. Make amends and learn lessons.

There is also something healthy about mistakes: as long as they are discovered and corrected, they can cleanse you of your big mistakes like a body cleansing. That’s why we have a professional review team review every piece of code.

9. Work in the present moment.

Everyone loves to discuss what could or should have been. It’s important to remember the past, and it’s certainly more meaningful to look to the future, but the present is the most important moment because that’s when all the work must be done.

10. You can do better.

Whatever you can do, you can do better. Keep learning and improving yourself.
