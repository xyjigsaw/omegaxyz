Dark Forest: The Past and Present of the Knowledge Graph

TechnologyIdeasMachine Learning随笔Developer LifeKnowledge GraphsIdeasKnowledge Graphs

dark forest

If he finds other life, there is only one thing he can do: shoot to eliminate it. In this forest, other people are hell and an eternal threat. I'll wait for you at the end of the world

Throwing away the morality of the dark forest, structurally speaking, knowledge is not an endless forest. The number of ups and downs in consciousness is numerous, and the universe is infinite. Philosophers thousands of years ago recognized the insignificance of human beings in the cosmic dimension. Similarly, knowledge is so profound that no one can fully understand it even if they spend their whole life.

Knowledge is like a circle (the black box is human knowledge, the colored circle is the knowledge of a Ph.D., and the rectangular box is the contribution of a Ph.D. to knowledge)

FortunatelyKnowledge GraphIt provides a clever way for humans to effectively obtain high-value information in the forest of knowledge. In this issue, the author will take you on a ride on a water droplet to have a glimpse of the past and present of the Knowledge Graph.

big Bang

When the first artificial computer ENIAC came out, the concept of data came to the fore, and its scale grew at a rate of 50% per year (doubtful). Throughout the history of the universe, counting from the data that humans can record to the present, it only takes a blink of an eye. In the 21st century, the popularization of big data has put forward higher requirements for the organization, mining and application of data. Similar to the grand unified theory that Einstein dreamed of, the data science community uses the construction of knowledge graphs to uniformly model various problems generated by data.A knowledge graph is essentially a graph structure consisting of nodes and edges. A node represents an entity and an edge represents a relationship., so we can also regard the knowledge graph as a collection of triples. Knowledge graph is not a new concept. It can be traced back to the Semantic Network [1] of the last century. Its original purpose was to convert text links in the World Wide Web into a semantic network to represent entity links. In 2012, Google added the knowledge graph to the search engine, using semantic retrieval to collect information from multiple sources to improve the quality of Google search.

google search engine

The construction of a knowledge graph is a systematic project. From original data to the formation of a knowledge graph, it goes through steps such as knowledge extraction, knowledge fusion (entity alignment), data model construction, and quality assessment.

Knowledge graph architecture process

warp spaceship

There are countless planets, stars and galaxies in the universe. Human beings can feel their existence with their eyes. However, due to the constraints of the speed of light and time, it is impossible to achieve interstellar flight and truly establish contact with them. On the other hand, humanity's current technology is blocked by sophons (doubtful), further reducing the possibility of successful research on curvature spacecraft. In the category of data, knowledge graph entity relationship extraction technology plays a key role as the curvature spacecraft, establishing connections in a large amount of unstructured data. For example, there is a text: "Hong Kong returned to China on July 1, 1997." Through entity relationship extraction, two entities can be obtained: Hong Kong, China, and one relationship: Belong, which constitutes a knowledge graph triplet <Hong Kong, Belongs to, China>. Entity relationship extraction is a classic task. Feature engineering, kernel methods, and graph models have been widely used in it, and some staged results have been achieved. With the advent of the deep learning era, neural network models have brought new breakthroughs to entity relationship extraction [2]. After the knowledge extraction step, we obtained the corresponding triples, and nominally completed the construction of the knowledge graph.

parallel universe

In many cases, a certain aspect of the knowledge graph exists independently. Different fields and different languages ​​will produce different knowledge graphs at different times. For example, Wikipedia and Baidu Encyclopedia can construct two kinds of knowledge graphs. In addition, there are also scholar knowledge graphs, movie knowledge graphs, etc. Each map is an independent universe, and knowledge fusion technology allows knowledge to freely shuttle in parallel universes [4]. The purpose of knowledge fusion is to merge information from different data sources, including matching and fusion of the data pattern layer and the data layer. It is mainly divided into the following three parts
  • Ontology Matching
  • Entity Alignment
  • Conflict detection and disambiguation (Disambiguation)
Ontology matching focuses on discovering equivalent or similar classes, attributes or relationships at the pattern level, also known as ontology mapping and ontology alignment; entity alignment focuses on discovering different instances that refer to the same object in the real world, also known as entity resolution and instance matching. In the field of ontology matching and entity alignment, traditional methods fuse different objects by designing corresponding similarity calculation algorithms. Currently, due to the rise of network representation learning, entity alignment based on embedding representation (refer to the next section) has become a current research hotspot. Knowledge graph fusion technology improves the quality of data and provides feasibility for building large-scale knowledge networks.

Dimensionality reduction strike

Two-way foil is one of the cosmic law weapons used by the Singer civilization. It can make one dimension in space infinitely curl up, thus forcing the three-dimensional universe and all matter in it to collapse into the two-dimensional universe. It is specially used to eliminate those weak civilizations that have interstellar navigation capabilities but do not yet possess curvature technology.

singer

Similar to the principle of two-way foil, Knowledge Graph Embedding is also a dimensionality reduction technology that aims to map entities and relationships into a low-dimensional dense vector space.

Knowledge graph embedding (here is a diagram of the network embedding algorithm DeepWalk)

It is a subset of network embeddings and a branch of representation learning. Currently, knowledge graph embedding includes two major categories: knowledge embedding that fuses factual information and fusion of additional information [3]. The former includes two subcategories, one based on translation (translation) distance model and the other based on semantic matching model.

Overview of knowledge graph embedding technology

After obtaining the embedding representation of triples, we can design a rich variety of downstream tasks through these embedding vectors. For example, entity alignment, link prediction (including knowledge graph completion technology), entity classification, question and answer system and recommendation system, etc., have improved the application scope of knowledge graph to a certain extent and become a key point for the implementation of knowledge graph technology.
