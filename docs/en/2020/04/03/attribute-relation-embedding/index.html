The difference between knowledge graph attributes and relationships

TechnologyMachine LearningKnowledge GraphsKnowledge Graphs
The difference between attributes and relationships in knowledge graphs mainly lies in the entities they face. There are two types of entity relationships, one is attribute and the other is relationship. The biggest difference is that the two entities corresponding to the triplet where the attribute is located are often a topic and a string, such as the attribute Type/Gender, the corresponding triplet (Justin Bieber, Type, Person), while the two entities corresponding to the triplet where the relationship is located are often two topics. For example, the relationship PlaceOfBrith, the corresponding triplet (Justin Bieber, PlaceOfBrith, London). For example, Tencent has the attribute of geographical location, and the value of this attribute is Beijing; if Beijing is defined as a string, I will say that the attribute of Tencent's geographical location is Beijing. If Beijing is instantiated as a node, it can be understood that Tencent has a relationship with Beijing, and the relationship is: located in. That is, if it is a string, it is actually an atrribute, used to represent the intrinsic attributes of an object or entity; in fact, Beijing is an entity, and "located in" at this time is the relationship between the two entities, called relationship, which is the external association of the entities. Therefore, whether it is an attribute or a relation depends on the nature of the connected value (literal or entity), and it must also be distinguished based on its internal performance or external correlation. When it is determined that the relationship is external, if the value is found to be literal, then it actually means that it needs to be materialized. vice versa.
