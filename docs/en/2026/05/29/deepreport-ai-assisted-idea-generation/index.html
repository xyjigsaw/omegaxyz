DeepReport: An AI-Assisted Idea Generation System for Scientific Research

TechnologyMachine LearningNatural Language ProcessingKnowledge GraphsArtificial IntelligenceLarge Language ModelsAcademic Big DataIdea GenerationDeepReport

DeepReport is a scientific idea generation system introduced in the SIGIR 2025 demo paper DeepReport: An AI-assisted Idea Generation System for Scientific Research. The paper starts from a concrete research bottleneck: academic literature has grown beyond what individual researchers can read, compare, and synthesize, especially when useful ideas require connections across distant fields.

The Core Tasks

The paper formulates idea generation as two sequential tasks. The first is Temporal Link Prediction: DeepReport analyzes evolving concept co-occurrence graphs to identify concept pairs that may become connected in the future. The second is Idea Verbalization: the system uses large language models to turn those structured concept links into coherent textual descriptions that researchers can inspect and refine.

This decomposition matters. Asking an LLM to “come up with a new idea” often produces generic suggestions. DeepReport first grounds generation in academic graph structure and temporal evidence, then uses the LLM as a verbalization layer. In other words, the system tries to make idea generation traceable to patterns in the literature rather than relying on free-form prompting alone.

How DeepReport Organizes Knowledge

DeepReport maintains evolving concept co-occurrence graphs over more than 260 million publications across disciplines, with concepts and relations updated periodically. The graph is not just a keyword index. It provides a structural background for questions such as which concepts are moving closer, which cross-domain relations are emerging, and where hidden connections may exist.

The system also integrates up-to-date academic databases and visualizes dynamic relationships among concepts. Within this architecture, graph analysis discovers patterns, temporal link prediction proposes candidate connections, and large language models translate those connections into readable research ideas. Together, these components make DeepReport closer to a research exploration workspace than a conventional paper search or summarization tool.

Why It Fits Scientific Research

The hardest part of interdisciplinary research is often not finding more papers, but identifying connections that are meaningful, defensible, and worth testing. DeepReport systematizes the path from literature scale to concept links to research hypotheses. It can reduce the cost of exploring a large knowledge landscape while still leaving the final evaluation to researchers.

That distinction is important: DeepReport should not be read as replacing scientific judgment. Its role is to expand the search space and provide structured candidates, evidence trails, and textual drafts. The actual research value still depends on human decisions about problem significance, feasibility, evidence strength, and experimental design.
