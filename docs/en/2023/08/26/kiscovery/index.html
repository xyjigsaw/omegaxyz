Evaluation

We use machine measurements and manual assessments to assess the systems we propose. We use the traditional confusion matrix to assess the performance of the model on the evolving concept of convergence. We have split the projections into two parts, one for all nodes in 2021 and the other for the new side only. As can be seen, all these models were able to identify most of the parties that existed in 2021, but GCN-GAN and EvolveGCN did not perform well when they discovered the new ones in 2021. We believe that this is because most of the maps are thin, which leads to the overcompatibility of other models. In our knowledge of the scenes, it is in finding new sides that the most important. We use the Turing test plus case analysis to validate the results of the generation model. We have recruited more specialists and non-specialists in the fields of computer science, geosciences (geology and geography) and medicine to conduct the Turin tests. Experts include professors, lecturers, post-doctoral researchers and graduate students (at least two professors per subject). Participants were asked to read the output generated by the machine and the text written manually, and to select the real manual text from a group of Ns. Each participant is instructed prior to testing. We also allow participants to use Internet search technical terms during testing. For each discipline, there are two different options, one with two options and the other with three options. The Turing test results are as follows: Our case studies deal with computer science, economics and political science. In the first case, we found a new conceptual convergence, namely, “knowledge mapping” and “comparison learning”, and produced a narrative based on a five-fold set of ideas that is now quoted. In the second case, we found that intellectual property rights were closely related to income distribution and produced a narrative based on the five-dollar group currently cited. In the last case, we found a gender pay gap in developing countries, particularly during the economic crisis, and we produced a narrative based on the five-tier group currently cited. These case studies show that our systems can predict and express the convergence of new concepts well and produce results consistent with human instincts and values.

DeepReport

Based on the above technology and objectives, we have developed the DeepReport Knowledge Discovery Research Auxiliary System, accessible.idea.acemap.cnCome and try. This is our first version of Pipeline, which consists mainly of three modules:
  • Data retrieval and graphic construction module
  • Time series link prediction module
  • Idea Generation Module
It first receives queries from users and retrieves the most relevant papers from the database and constructs in real time a common map of evolutionary concepts. At the same time, the system maintains two dictionaries that are used to store mapping relationships between papers and concepts. Summary

In conclusion, the paper proposes a conceptually based framework for intellectual inspiration, which has been integrated into a research assistant system. We have built an evolving conceptual convergence map and are exploring potential linkages between different concepts using a time-link prediction methodology based on masked language models. In order to express the newly discovered links, we have produced a narrative based on a five-dollar set of references using pre-trained language models. Our system has been evaluated through automatic measurements and manual assessments. We believe that this system can help researchers to discover new ideas more quickly and play an important role in academic research. In the future, we will continue to improve our systems, including the expansion of data sets, the refinement of the conceptual extraction tool and the optimization of time-link forecasting methods, which will ultimately require that we use a large-language model to train multiple knowledge discovery missions to accomplish both of these objectives.
