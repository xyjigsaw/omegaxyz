Structural information modeling in knowledge graphs

TechnologyMachine LearningDeep LearningKnowledge Graphs神经网络Knowledge Graphs

What is a knowledge graph

Gartner, the world's most authoritative IT research and consulting company, released the 2020 technology maturity curve in the AI ​​field.Knowledge graphThe expectations are at the peak of the AI ​​field, and there are still 5 to 10 years for development opportunities to reach a plateau, that is, large-scale commercial use. The concept of knowledge graph became popular after the introduction of Google Knowledge Graph in 2012. Tracing its roots, the predecessor of knowledge graph is the semantic network. In layman's terms, a knowledge graph integrates disorderly and scattered information into knowledge in the form of a graph.So what is the difference between the concepts of knowledge graph, graph, and heterogeneous graph? Formal definitions of the three concepts are given here.A graph is a collection of nodes and edges. Social networks, citation networks, and context trees are all typical graphs. The second is the definition of heterogeneous graph. Heterogeneous graph can also be called heterogeneous information network. The main difference between it and graph is that HG has multiple node types or relationship types. Among them, ourAcemapThe academic knowledge graph is a heterogeneous graph. Therefore, the knowledge graph is a special heterogeneous graph. However, the knowledge graph focuses on fact-based triples. As can be seen from the definition, it is composed of entities, relationship types, and facts. The fact is that the triplet based on the relationship between the head node and the tail node is essentially the edge of the entity. It should be noted that in KG, the number and type of edges between two entities are arbitrary. Therefore, structurally speaking, the relationships and paths between node types in knowledge graphs are exponential, far beyond heterogeneous graphs. That's why I put ourAcemapattributed to heterogeneous graphs. Interestingly, although knowledge graphs are heterogeneous graphs, researchers in these two directions hardly cite each other's papers.

Knowledge graph structure concept

Schema

Knowledge graphs and heterogeneous graphs have some shared concepts in structure. The first one isschema [1]. It is a graph composed of node types and relationship types, independent and actual nodes, reflecting information at the knowledge concept level. On the far left is a heterogeneous graph of an academic network, andAcemapSimilarly, it includes various types of entities such as authors and papers.

Meta-path

Another structural concept ismetapath. The meta path refers to the path between node types on the schema. Since there are many types of node relationships in the knowledge graph, the number of meta-paths is also growing exponentially. Therefore, in the field of representation learning of KG, meta-paths of all nodes are rarely used to model. However, we can regard meta-paths as a reasoning process. In the reasoning tasks of KG, meta-paths are very valuable and can be applied to specific tasks such as question answering systems and recommendation systems.

Knowledge graph structure information modeling

Among them, using the structural information in KG for representation learning is the most researched direction. Obviously, the modeling of structural information is nothing more than three parts: edges, paths and subgraphs. Most of these studios are based on edges, and a small part of the work is modeled using paths in the knowledge graph (this path is a real path rather than a meta-path). There are almost no KG representation learning methods that purely use subgraph modeling.

Link-based

Different from GNN based on message passing, since there are many types of entities and relationships in KG, its representation learning is modeled by designing a triple scoring function instead of using meta-paths.There are three classic models for embedding based on first-order structural information, that is, edge-based embedding methods [2]. Among them, transE generates embedding vectors of triples, and through the scoring function, these vectors satisfy the properties of head entity + relationship = tail entity. However, it can only handle 1-to-1 relationships and cannot handle 1-to-many-many-to-many-to-many relationships. transH and transR respectively map entities and relationships to different hyperplanes or coordinate systems to solve such problems.

Path-based

PtransE [3] is a path-based model. Different from transE, it takes the path information between entities into consideration in the modeling process. As can be seen from the figure, the author turns the path into a composite relationship, so that these vectors meet the requirements of head entity + composite relationship = tail entity for training. The energy function reflects this process. At the same time, considering that different paths have different contributions, this model scores each path by the number of branches in the path. The fewer the number of branches, the higher the reliability of the path. This is reflected in the second formula, and is finally trained through the negative sampling loss function.

Subgraph-based

As mentioned earlier, there are very few papers that use subgraphs for modeling, and there are only some cross-cutting works, such as CoLAKE [4] here, which is a model that trains natural language and knowledge at the same time. It first connects the words in the text into a complete graph, and then finds the subgraph of entities in the text from the knowledge graph, and merges the complete graph of the text with the subgraph of KG. It can achieve good results on many natural language tasks.

other

So how do we integrate more specific structural information into representation learning? We first see an example in graph embedding. This is a paper this year, which is a neural network GNN model based on VNGE, called VNEstruct [5].In the figure, the one-hop neighborhood of a node is dark yellow, and the second-hop neighborhood is light yellow. This model sets a radius R for each node. Before training, within this radius R, the VNGE of the 1-hop neighborhood subgraph is calculated to the VNGE of the R-hop domain subgraph. Subsequently, the author combines these R values ​​into a vector for training. But I don't think this is a perfect model. A perfect model should take the entire graph as input and extract features through the neural network for training. The VNEstrcut model performs feature engineering on the nodes before input, and inputs the manually extracted VNGE vector into the neural network. Therefore a good model should be an end-to-end model, because the neural network is essentially a feature extractor that selectively extracts the structural characteristics of the graph according to the specific task.
