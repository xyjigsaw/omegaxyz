A researcher's h-index means that he has at most h papers that have been cited at least h times. The h-index can more accurately reflect a person's academic achievements. The higher a person's h-index is, the more influential his paper is. For example, if someone has an h-index of 20, this means that he has published 20 papers that have each been cited at least 20 times. It is very easy to determine a person's h-index. Go to the SCI website and find out all the SCI papers published by a person, sort them from high to low by the number of citations, and check downward until the serial number of a certain paper is greater than the number of citations of the paper. Subtract 1 from that serial number to get the h-index. Hawking, who is more familiar to Chinese readers, has a relatively high h-index of 62. Among biologists, the highest h-index is Schneider, a neurobiologist at Johns Hopkins University and winner of the Wolf Prize in Medicine, with a score of 191, followed by Baltimore, a biologist at the California Institute of Technology and winner of the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine, with 160. Biologists' h-indexes are all on the high side, indicating that the h-index, like other indicators,Not suitable for cross-disciplinary comparisons.
