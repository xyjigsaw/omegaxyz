Time value of money and cost-effectiveness of risky software projects

技术域ideas程序人生软件工程ideas软件工程

1. Introduction

Software engineering economics involves various engineering economic activities in software projects and products, such as software project investment and financing, bidding and tendering, project feasibility analysis, project task decomposition, plan schedule designation and team organization building, software project costs, pricing, benefits, and the correlation analysis and statistical content between engineering economic parameters such as economic and social effect evaluation, risk analysis, and software testing.[1]. The R&D and management of any software are carried out within a specific time and space, and clarifying the concept of time value of funds is of great significance to the development of software projects and enterprises. If decision-makers ignore the time value of money factor, they will not be able to correctly calculate the revenue and expenditure of software projects in different periods, let alone evaluate the profits and losses of investment activities.[2]. In recent years, many information technology companies have long neglected the application of the time value of funds theory, resulting in low efficiency in the use of funds, which has caused many software development to be abandoned halfway, abandoning the basics and ultimately facing the risk of transformation failure and decision-making errors. For example, Renren, Happy Farm, Smartisan Technology, China Mobile Fetion, China Yahoo, etc.[3]. These projects have long R&D cycles, slow capital recovery, and poor investment results. Therefore, in order for Internet companies to develop better, it is necessary to introduce the time value of money into software research and development, and consider this factor in financial management and production and operation activities. This is the focus of this article.

2. Overview of the time value of money theory

The time value of funds: refers to the appreciation of funds over time. It is the increase in value after the turnover of funds. It is not only affected by the inflation compensation rate, but also by the investment profit rate and risk compensation rate.[4]. Therefore, the capital values ​​at different time points cannot be added directly and must be added after equivalent operations. Figure 1 shows the final value formula diagram of the one-time payment type equivalent conversion. P is the present value, F is the future value, the interest rate is i, and the time is n years.

Figure 1 Final value formula diagram of lump sum equivalent conversion

Discounting: refers to converting all cash amounts occurring at different points in time into the amount at the current time t=0. This conversion process is called "discounting". The value of funds discounted at a future point in time to the current point in time is called "present value"[5]. Discount rate: refers to the rate that converts future limited-term expected income into present value. The principalization rate and capitalization rate or reduction interest rate usually refer to the ratio that converts the future indefinite expected income into the present value. The discount rate is not an interest rate, nor a discount rate, but a rate of return. The determination of the discount rate is usually closely related to the current interest rate level.[5]. Cash flow: Funds flowing into or out of the project system within a certain period of time (the life of the project). Among them, cash receipts are called cash inflows (positive); cash disbursements are called cash outflows (negative), and the difference between cash inflows and cash outflows is called net cash flow. In order to accurately express the cash flow of a project or investment plan, the investment project or investment plan should be regarded as an independent system. 3.1 Basic principles of cost-benefit analysis

Cost-benefit analysis is a method to evaluate the value of a project by comparing all costs and benefits of the project. As an economic decision-making method, cost-benefit analysis applies the cost-expense analysis method to the planning and decision-making of government departments to seek how to obtain the maximum benefits with the minimum cost in investment decisions. Often used to assess the value of public utility projects where social benefits need to be quantified. Managers in non-public industries can also use this method to analyze the intangible benefits (Soft benefits) of a large project. 3.2 Software cost analysis

Costs are first classified according to the stage at which they occur in the project life cycle. It can be mainly divided into the following three categories.
  • (1) Software-defined costs: including feasibility analysis, demand analysis, etc.
  • (2) Software development costs: including hardware support for software development, wages and other employment costs of employees involved in development projects, and all related costs.
  • (3) Operation and maintenance costs: This part of the cost accounts for the largest proportion, accounting for software R&D[8]75% of the total cost or even more.

3.4 Analysis based on net present value

The basic idea of ​​the net present value method is to convert the cash flows of investment (expenditure) and income (income) in different periods during the life of the software project to the reference point t=O (i.e., discount) based on the time value of funds, and accumulate this series of discounted values, and define it as the net present value (NPV) of the project's cash flow.[7]. If the NPV is greater than zero, it means that the project is feasible from the perspective of economic benefits; if the NPV is less than or equal to zero, it means that the software project is not feasible from the perspective of economic benefits and is not worth investing in. 3.5 Other methods

Among the methods of economic benefit analysis of large software projects, the internal rate of return method and the investment payback period method are also included. The internal rate of return method is a method that solves the corresponding critical discount rate i when the project plan reaches breakeven.t, and convert itCompared with the benchmark discount rate or the minimum expected profitability rate (determined by the investor)itAn evaluation method that determines whether a project is worth investing in by comparison. The so-called investment payback period of an engineering project refers to the time during which the investment in the engineering project is fully recovered in the form of net income. It reflects the speed (in years) at which project investment can be compensated (return). The investment payback period method is an evaluation method that determines the feasibility of the investment project by solving the investment payback period of a given engineering project and comparing it with the industry benchmark investment payback period. Commonly used are the static payback period method and the dynamic payback period method. For the specific operational measures of the above two methods, please refer to the paper [1] and will not be repeated here.

4. Conclusion

In a large and high-risk software project, analysis cannot simply be done statically at a time state. The correct approach should be to conduct a risk and cost-benefit analysis based on the time value of money at every stage of the software life cycle. If a certain economic indicator changes, you need to be prepared in advance. Once the analysis finds that the software project is not feasible, the development of the software project should be stopped immediately to prevent the enterprise from suffering greater losses.
