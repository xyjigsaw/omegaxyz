Research on human-computer interaction methods based on eye tracking technology

TechnologyMachine LearningDeep Learning计算机视觉神经网络CNNHCI计算机视觉
From scientific research to commercial applications, eye tracking technology has received widespread attention. This article mainly discusses the development process, application fields, theoretical basis, research methods and development prospects of eye tracking technology. Through research, it is believed that eye tracking technology has extremely high requirements on the real-time and accuracy of computer hardware and software. Due to the development of deep learning in recent years, eye tracking technology has greatly improved its ability to handle complex problems, and is scalable and practical for large-scale commercial applications.Keywords: eye tracking technology; human-computer interaction; multi-channel; neural network; virtual reality

introduction

With the rapid development of computer hardware and software technology, multi-modal human-computer interaction (MMHCI) has become a very popular field in human-computer interaction software engineering methods at home and abroad. Since multi-channel human-computer interaction is more in line with people's psychological and physiological habits, it is considered a more natural way of human-computer interaction.[1]. Different from traditional single-channel interaction methods such as WIMP/GUI, multi-channel human-computer interaction methods can obtain information from multiple channels, process information and generate feedback to users through the fusion of multi-channel information. These channels include speech recognition, face recognition, emotion understanding, gesture understanding, posture analysis, pen, haptics, etc.[2]. At the same time, channels can complement each other, overcome each other's shortcomings, and have stronger expression ability, naturalness, flexibility and portability. The human-computer interaction method is closer to the human-to-human interaction method. By analyzing this input information using artificial intelligence and machine learning technologies, computers can effectively capture the user's intentions and improve the naturalness and efficiency of human-computer interaction. Research on multi-channel user interface, which represents a new paradigm of human-computer interaction. The eyes are the windows to the soul, through which we can explore the laws of many psychological activities of people. Human information processing relies heavily on vision. About 80% to 90% of information from the outside world is obtained through human eyes.[3], Human visual perception information can be obtained by tracking the gaze point of the human eye. Therefore, scientific research on "how people see things" has never stopped. In this regard, the study of eye tracking is considered to be the most effective method in the study of visual information processing.

definition

Eye tracking is a scientific application technology that allows users to send information and receive feedback without physical contact with interactive devices. In principle, eye tracking mainly studies the acquisition, modeling and simulation of eye movement information, and has a wide range of uses. In addition to infrared devices, the devices that obtain eye movement information can also be image acquisition devices, or even cameras on ordinary computers or mobile phones, which can also achieve eye tracking with the support of software.[4]。

development process

The concept of eye tracking first emerged in the 1930s. It was originally designed for commercial considerations. However, due to limitations in sensor technology and information processor computing speed at that time, eye tracking technology encountered many difficulties in its development and developed very slowly.[4]. As eye tracking technology matures, the applications of eye trackers become more and more widespread. Foreign countries have begun to develop eye trackers at the beginning of the 20th century. Now eye tracking technology has developed relatively well, and many companies have developed eye trackers into finished products. According to the results published on the eye tracker database website established by Professor David Woodillg of the Institute of Behavioral Science at the University of Derby in the UK, there are currently about 44 manufacturers of eye trackers in the world. Eye trackers designed based on the reflection principle of the cornea and pupil include: 504 and 501 eye trackers produced by the American Applied Science Laboratory (ASL), eyelink II helmet-mounted eye trackers produced by the Canadian SR company, Mon VOGl and Mon VOG2 eye trackers produced by the Me Division company in France, etc. In medicine, the VISX STAR S3 excimer laser system in the United States is a three-dimensional automatic eye tracking system. The system automatically adjusts the laser to the cutting point with eye movement to quickly and safely treat eye diseases. In addition, it can also check the function of the patient's brain smoothing system by recording eye movements, focusing on observing the steady eye tracking (SEEM) of patients with schizophrenia, manic depression, anxiety, and obsessive-compulsive disorder and the exploratory eye movement (SEMVF) when observing graphics. In recent years, with the development of optical sensor technology and the improvement of computer information processing capabilities, computers can process complex image information in real time. At the same time, CCD technology has developed rapidly from linear array to area array, which has led to the rapid development of eye tracking technology. There are also more and more studies on the visual eye movement system. Famous scientific research institutions or universities such as NASA, Harvard, Massachusetts, Cambridge, and Oxford have dedicated visual eye movement system research departments. The addition of deep learning has also injected new vitality into the development of eye tracking technology.

Current application

In civilian applications, eye tracking is mainly used in various fields such as medicine, interface design and evaluation, product testing (advertising testing, web page testing, product usability testing, etc.), scene research (mall shopping, store decoration, home environment, etc.), dynamic analysis (aerospace-related fields, sports, automobiles, aircraft driving, typing action analysis, etc.) and human-computer interaction. In addition, it also has good application prospects in the fields of intelligent computers that understand human intentions, home appliances with interactive functions, virtual reality, and games.

Interface design

In terms of interface design, some internal mechanisms of human information processing during the interaction between humans and computers can be revealed by analyzing the user's gaze scanning path, and the quantitative indicators obtained from eye movement testing can be used to establish a prototype test model, so that the conceptual model of the interface matches the human psychological model. In terms of interface evaluation, some important eye movement parameters can be obtained by collecting data related to the spatiotemporal characteristics of eye movements and analyzing the data, thereby providing a basis for optimizing interface design.

product testing

In terms of product testing, taking advertising testing as an example, the eye tracker is mainly used to examine some eye movement characteristics of people when watching advertisements, such as the order in which customers look at the advertising screen, the time spent looking at any part of the advertisement, the number of gazes, saccades, changes in pupil diameter, etc., and then the advertising effect is tested by combining it with the memory test.

Efficacy

In terms of ergonomics, in modern human-machine systems, especially in aerospace driving, operators operate and manage complex systems and mechanical equipment in a specific environment. When people work in this environment, they must not only rely on their eyes to observe the environment, but also rely on careful attention to complete precise control actions. Using the eye tracker system, by analyzing the movement of the eyes, we can know how people allocate their attention during operation. At the same time, we can understand how to design and rationally distribute instruments, screens, and external views to achieve the best human-computer interaction, which not only reduces the operator's workload, avoids errors, and effectively improves ergonomics. In addition, Eriksson obtained fatigue status by tracking, locating and identifying eye status. Grace developed a drowsiness monitoring system for heavy truck drivers based on the eyelid closure method. Singh and Zheng used facial recognition technology for fatigue detection.

human-computer interaction

In terms of human-computer interaction, the purpose of human-computer interaction is to use all possible information channels for human-computer communication and improve the naturalness and efficiency of interaction. The visual input method uses sight to operate the computer while fully tracking the line of sight in real time. It is used to help severely disabled people who can only control eye muscles control, communicate, entertain, etc. Canon's new camera model (EOS2E) adds a new eye-controlled focusing function that can focus based on the person's gaze position in a picture. This function is achieved by adding a one-dimensional eye movement measurement device to the camera. Similarly, there are some systems that allow users to use their eyes to control the mouse to draw, etc.

Aviation and Military

In terms of aviation and military affairs, with the strong demand for high technology in modern warfare, helmet-mounted displays and helmet-mounted sights (HMD/HMS), as an important airborne display equipment, have become increasingly prominent in their status and role. Stagger D/Bungee S are increasingly being used in some new fighter aircraft for tracking targets within the field of view. Since the rotation of the eyes is fast and flexible and not limited by physiological factors, as long as the target is seen within the field of vision, the fighter aircraft can capture the target, which can gain valuable time in the fight and increase the chance of victory. Therefore, if the eyes can be unaffected and can rotate flexibly and measure the direction of the eye's sighting line in real time, so that the fighter plane can track the target, lock the target and attack the target more quickly, the sensitivity and accuracy of the fighter plane's attack can be greatly improved, thereby greatly improving the combat capability and survivability of the fighter plane. The U.S. Air Force's virtual cockpit program in the 1980s used many different information sources to form a "virtual world" around pilots. Eye tracking technology was listed as a technology in the program as a supplement to the helmet sight. In 1994, this technology was regarded as a military secret technology by the U.S. Air Force and began to be applied to some new fighter aircraft, such as F-16, YF-23, F-22, F-104, F/A-18, F-111, etc.   In summary, eye tracking is an important tool in many fields, from scientific research to commercial applications. Despite its wide range of applications, eye tracking has yet to become a ubiquitous technology. in the latest research areas[5,6,8], some researchers have developed eye tracking software on commercial hardware such as mobile phones and tablets, so that everyone can have eye tracking capabilities in the palm of their hand without the need for additional sensors or equipment.

Main research method classification

The main implementation methods of eye tracking technology can be divided into two categories: hardware-based and software-based. Among hardware-based tracking technologies, gaze tracking devices include intrusiveness and no-intrusiveness, wearable and non-wearable.[9], contact lenses and non-contact lenses; their accuracy ranges from 0.1 degrees to 1 degree or 2 degrees, and the manufacturing costs also vary greatly. This method requires the user to wear a special helmet, special contact lenses, or use a head holder, a camera placed above the user's head, etc. This method has high recognition accuracy, but it is very disruptive to the user. The accuracy of eye tracking and the restrictions and interference on users are an irreconcilable contradiction. In order to overcome the interference of eye tracking devices on people, in recent years people have proposed software-based eye tracking methods that do not interfere with users. The basic working principle is to first use a camera to obtain images of human eyes or faces, and then use image processing algorithms to detect, locate and track faces and eyes in the images, thereby estimating the user's gaze position (computer vision). The framework of standard eye tracking methods for image processing is shown in Figure 1.After collecting the user's eye image through the video camera, the image needs to be filtered, binarized and other image preprocessing[12], providing a basis for subsequent eye movement tracking. In addition, many scholars use neural network methods to process image information to obtain accurate eye tracking feedback. Finally, in the development process of eye tracking technology, many scientists have continued to research and explore, and creatively proposed some methods of tracking eye movements. From the initial direct observation method and mechanical recording method to the later electromagnetic induction method, current recording method and contact lens method, corneal reflection tracking method, double Pukin field method, iris-scleral edge tracking technology, pupil-cornea tracking method, etc. The specific principle is as follows: (1) Electromagnetic induction method: Anesthetize the subject's eyes, and attach a contact lens equipped with a detection coil to the eye. There is an induced voltage in the coil, and through phase-sensitive detection of the induced voltage, eye movements in the horizontal and vertical directions can be accurately measured. This method is highly accurate, but contact with the eyeballs can cause discomfort to the subject. (2) Current recording method: Eye movements can produce bioelectrical phenomena. The metabolism of the cornea and the retina is different. The metabolic rate of the cornea is smaller, while the metabolic rate of the omentum is larger. Therefore, a potential difference is formed between the cornea and the omentum. The cornea is positively charged and the omentum is negatively charged. A stable baseline potential can be recorded when the eyes are looking forward without eye movement. When the eyes move in the horizontal direction, the potential difference between the skin on the left and right sides of the eyes changes. When the eyes move in the vertical direction, the potential differences on the upper and lower sides of the eyes change. Placing two pairs of silver chloride skin surface electrodes on the left and right, upper and lower sides of the eye can cause weak electrical signals in the changing direction of the eyeball, and after amplification, the position information of the eyeball movement can be obtained. This method is characterized by high bandwidth, low accuracy, and high interference to people. (3) Contact lens method: First, a small mirror is attached to the subject's eye, and light is directed to the mirror. The reflected light changes with the movement of the eyeball, thereby obtaining eye movement signals. Its technical characteristics are the highest accuracy, high bandwidth, large interference to people, and making people feel uncomfortable. (4) Corneal reflection tracking method: Because the cornea protrudes from the surface of the eyeball, the reflection angle of the cornea to light from a fixed light source also changes during eye movement. Therefore, a near-infrared LED light source can be placed in front of the human eye, and a camera fixed directly in front of the subject's head. The light reflected by the cornea is transmitted to the camera through a beam separation device in front of the eye and some reflectors and lenses. The same device is placed in front of the other eye. The position of the cornea reflecting light is determined through the image on the screen of a camera fixed in front of the head and some corresponding algorithms. The largest errors in this system are mainly the slippage of the head optical system and errors caused by the distance between the eyes and the camera lens. (5) Double Pukinfield method: The Pukinfield image is an image formed by reflections from several optical interfaces of the eye. The image reflected by the cornea is the first Pukinje image, the image reflected from the back surface of the cornea is weaker and is called the second Pukinje image, the image reflected from the front surface of the lens is called the third Pukinje image, and the image reflected from the back surface of the lens is called the fourth Pukinje image. Eye gaze position can be determined by measuring two Pukinje images. Its technical characteristics are high precision, high bandwidth, and high interference to people. (6) Iris and sclera edge tracking technology: Invisible infrared light is used to illuminate the eye, and two infrared photosensitive tubes are installed near the eye. The infrared light reflected from the left and right parts of the edge of the iris and sclera is received by these two photosensitive tubes respectively. When the eyeball moves to the right, the iris turns to the right, and the infrared rays received by the right photosensitive tube will decrease; while the reflection part of the left sclera increases, causing the infrared rays received by the left photosensitive tube to increase. This differential signal can be used to detect eye movements without contact. Its disadvantages are large errors and low vertical accuracy. (7) Pupil and cornea tracking method: The optical elements of the system are fixed in space and have a relatively fixed distance relative to the subject's eyes, and the reflected image is recorded with a camera. The data obtained by the camera is processed by a computer or microprocessor to identify the pupil and CR (cornea). Then, the cornea reflection point data is used as the base point for the relative position of the eye camera and the eyeball, and the gaze point in the screen space is calculated based on the pupil center position coordinates. This method is accurate, has small headgear error and does not interfere with people.[7,10]. The circle detection method based on Hough transform needs to search the three parameters circle center, radius, etc. in the parameter space. First, the integral projection method is used to detect the approximate position of the eye pupil, then the edge detection operator is used to extract the edge information of the image, and the edge image is binarized, and then the circle-based Hough transform is used to quickly locate the human eye.

Limitations and development prospects

Limitations of eye tracking technology in human-computer interaction

Since eye tracking technology is not yet mature, different eye tracking technologies have their own advantages and disadvantages, such as sampling rate, accuracy, and interference. Therefore, there are still certain limitations in applying it to human-computer interaction. The main problems include accuracy and freedom, and the "Midas contact" problem.[15]and algorithmic issues. The first is the issue of accuracy and degrees of freedom. Compared with software-based gaze tracking technology, hardware-based gaze tracking technology has higher accuracy, but because the equipment used limits people's freedom, it interferes with people greatly and is not very convenient to use. Software-based gaze tracking technology, although reducing restrictions on users, has a much lower accuracy, making it difficult to obtain an accurate focus of attention. Accuracy and freedom are currently in sharp contradiction. Second is the issue of "Midas Contact". The movement of the user's gaze is often random and does not always have a certain meaning. Moving the gaze does not mean that a computer command must be issued. Therefore, if the computer mouse pointer on the screen always moves with the user's line of sight, it is likely to annoy the user. This problem can be solved well if the interface can process the user's gaze input in a timely manner when the user wishes to issue control, and ignore the movement of the gaze when the user wishes to issue control. Unfortunately, it is generally impossible to distinguish between the two situations[5,7]. Again, it’s an algorithm problem. Jitter and blinking during eye movement can easily cause data interruption. This interference signal makes it difficult to obtain the screen projection of the focus of attention and the eye movement data. For the first problem, the screen object closest to the gaze point can be regarded as the object of interest to the user. However, due to the low accuracy of the gaze point itself, it is difficult to judge when the screen elements are close. For the latter problem, some prior models of eye movements are currently being used to compensate. However, there is currently no mature and efficient fusion method that can integrate the visual channel with other channels to achieve seamless connection. In this article, the blinking situation is considered in the detection part of the pupil contour feature points, and the threshold is set for judgment. Once the threshold value is exceeded, it is considered that a data interruption has occurred, and the processing of the next frame of image begins.

Development prospects of eye tracking technology based on neural networks

In recent years, due to the development of deep learning, eye tracking technology based on neural networks has also developed.[6,11]. Studies have shown that efficient eye-tracking software can work reliably on mobile devices such as smartphones and tablets without requiring any external accessories (Figure 3). Using eye-tracking technology on mobile devices offers many benefits: (1) widespread use of smartphones by more than a third of the world's population, estimated in 2019, far exceeding the number of desktop or laptop users; (2) high adoption rates of technology upgrades - a large portion of the population With the latest hardware, computationally expensive methods such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs) can be used in real time; (3) the heavy use of cameras on mobile devices has led to the rapid development and deployment of camera technology, (4) the fixed position of the camera relative to the screen reduces the number of unknown parameters.

Eye-tracking-based virtual reality technology

Virtual reality technology uses computers to generate a simulated environment (such as aircraft cockpit, operation site, etc.), and allows users to "invest" in the environment through a variety of sensing devices. Technology that enables users to interact directly and naturally with the environment is a computer system technology that can create and experience virtual environments[13]. To create a more immersive experience, VR headset companies are investing heavily in eye-tracking technology. In fact, in a sense, eye tracking technology can be seen as a complement to VR technology[14]. In VR, eye tracking technology should be used more in background work in the future, which may include the following areas: (1) Graphics rendering resource allocation: When a person looks somewhere, more graphics rendering resources can be allocated in this direction. This enhances rendering power and thus improves output quality. (2) Data prefetch: VR data processing generally takes a certain amount of time to complete, such as searching for data in an online database. If the user wants to scan in a certain direction, data prefetching can start in the background before the user selects an item. This improves the perception of VR environments and is particularly useful on mobile data networks. (3) Multimodal smart 3-D object selection: It is not easy to point to a small object in a cluttered VR environment. Eye tracking technology can make the object more specific and clear by combining eye movement information with the controller, so that the user can accurately select the object. (4) Automatic headset calibration: By determining the position of the user's eyeballs, the headset can better adjust the picture parameters and provide the user with the best comfort. (5) Balance manipulation: The vestibulo-ocular reflex is a neural reflex between eye movements and the vestibular system. If the information about eye movement and head-mounted display movement is obtained through the accelerometer, the possible state of the user's vestibular system can be judged. This allows for systematic operations to enhance the sense of balance and reduce the impact of motion sickness during VR use.

Review conclusion

Research on eye tracking technology is considered the most effective means in visual information processing research, attracting widespread attention in the fields of psychology, ergonomics, computer science and kinesiology. Its research results have been widely used in industry, commerce and military fields. Although eye tracking has been around for centuries, we believe that this work will become the next generation of key benchmark eye tracking solutions for human-computer interaction and serve the majority of people.
