Introduction

hereGithub repository, we chooseCOVID-19anddeep learningfield to predict scientific development trends. we useAceMapIt is worth noting that we also collected papers from the entire academic community, including 19 disciplines (mathematics, history, psychology, economics, sociology, art, geography, business, physics, political science, philosophy, biology, computer science, materials science, environmental science, medicine, chemistry, engineering, and geology) and 292 subfields (subdisciplines). To predict academic trends, we constructed a domain graph based on paper citations. Specifically, the papers we crawled all have domain tags. Each paper may belong to several areas. We use reference relationships to link these fields. For example, paper P1 belongs to fields F1 and F2, paper P2 belongs to fields F2 and F3, and similarly, P1 refers to P2. Then, we add edges (F1, F2), (F1, F3), (F2, F2) and (F2, F3), with the weight of each edge being the total number of citations in the domain. The ID of the node can be found on Acemap, for example the ID of the business is 2024422753, then you can view the field by visiting https://www.acemap.info/field/2024422753.

Statistics

We generated a dynamic field map of COVID-19 on a day-by-day basis from January 1, 2020 to March 31, 2021 (456 days). The time range of deep learning is from October 1, 2017 to March 31, 2021. Finally, a dynamic domain graph is constructed and called COVID-19 (deep learning) academic graph. You can start fromGoogle DriveDownload both datasets.

Introduction to analysis

MotifTrends

Cross-topic patterns are rising in both fields, with the number of cross-topics in deep learning far greater than COVID-19. Later in the pandemic, cross-trends slowed down and reached convergence. From the end of 2018 to 2019, the field of deep learning experienced explosive growth. Although the pace of deep learning paper publication has slowed down in recent months, there is still a lot of progress ahead compared to COVID-19.

transfer entropy

Transfer entropy is a nonparametric statistic that measures the amount of directional (temporally asymmetric) information transferred between two random processes. We use transfer entropy to mine the potential influence between different fields from the PageRank perspective. More details can be found in our paper.If you use this repository, please cite it.
